## 5. Transmutation Principles (Learned from CBR)

The CBR materials reveal the founder's investment communication philosophy. The Alchemist must maintain these principles:

1. **No fabricated projections.** CBR says "we will discover the pricing model." SG/Send should say the same -- do not invent revenue projections that have no empirical basis.
2. **Hypothesis-driven, not speculation-driven.** Every feature creates a billable unit. Revenue forecasts are based on tested hypotheses, not market-sizing extrapolation.
3. **The gold is real.** Every claim traces to a source document. CBR's materials are grounded in demonstrated capability, not aspirational positioning.
4. **Serverless = no kill date.** The financial narrative is not "how long until we run out of money" but "how do we grow from near-zero cost to revenue without runway pressure."
5. **Open source as competitive advantage.** Not a liability. "Anyone can verify our encryption" is stronger than "trust our proprietary implementation."
6. **Investor as user.** CBR positions investors as platform users, not just capital providers. SG/Send should do the same: "your first use of SG/Send will be receiving this pitch deck."
