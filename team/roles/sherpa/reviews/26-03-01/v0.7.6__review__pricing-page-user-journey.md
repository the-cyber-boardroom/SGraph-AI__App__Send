# Sherpa Review: Pricing Page & Home Page User Journey

**Version:** v0.7.6
**Date:** 2026-03-01
**Role:** Sherpa
**Scope:** User journey analysis across Home, Pricing, EAP, and Cloud Shared pages
**Goal:** Evaluate how effectively the pages nudge new users toward (a) joining the Early Access Programme or (b) buying credits (Starter Pack)

---

## Executive Summary

The pricing page architecture is well-structured and the copy is generally clear. There is a logical progression from Home to Pricing to action (EAP or Buy Credits). However, several friction points reduce conversion potential. The most critical issues are: (1) the home page hero CTA sends users directly to send.sgraph.ai where they will hit a token wall with no context, (2) the "Buy Credits" button on the Cloud Shared page opens a mailto link rather than a Stripe checkout, and (3) "zero-knowledge encryption" is used heavily but never explained in plain language for non-technical users. Below is a detailed analysis with specific, actionable recommendations.

---

## 1. User Journey Clarity

### Current Flow

```
Home Page                     Pricing Page                    Action Pages
-----------                   ---------------                 ---------------
Hero: "Try SG/Send" ------>   send.sgraph.ai (TOKEN WALL)

Nav: "Pricing" ----------->   Pricing hero
                              |
                              +-- EAP card -------->  early-access/ (form)
                              +-- Buy Credits ------>  cloud/ (details + mailto)
                              +-- Self-Hosted ------>  self-hosted/ (GitHub)
                              +-- Your Cloud ------->  your-cloud/ (guide)
                              +-- Partner ---------->  partners/
                              +-- Dedicated -------->  dedicated/ (contact)
                              +-- AWS Marketplace -->  aws-marketplace/
                              +-- Managed ---------->  managed/ (contact)
```

### What Works

- **Nav includes "Pricing" as a top-level item** -- easy to find from any page.
- **Pricing page is well-organised into two clear sections** -- "Free" and "SG/Send Cloud" -- with a visual separation (encryption callout) between them.
- **The "How it works" section on the pricing page** (4 steps: get token, upload, share, zero knowledge) is excellent. It gives a new user a concrete mental model of the workflow in under 30 seconds.
- **The "Why Free?" page** effectively addresses scepticism about free tiers and has strong CTAs at the bottom pointing to both EAP and Buy Credits.
- **The Early Access page** has a clean, focused signup form with minimal friction (name + email only) and clear expectations ("token within 48 hours").

### Problems

**P1 (Critical): Home page hero CTA is a dead end for new users.**
The primary hero CTA on the home page is "Try SG/Send" which links directly to `send.sgraph.ai`. A first-time visitor who clicks this will land on the send app, where they need a token to do anything. Without a token, they are stuck. There is no onboarding, no redirect back to pricing, and no explanation of how to get a token. This is the single biggest user journey failure.

**P2 (High): No "Get Started" or "Pricing" CTA in the home page hero.**
The two hero CTAs are "Try SG/Send" (broken for new users, see P1) and "Meet the Team" (internal curiosity, not conversion). Neither directs users toward the pricing page, the EAP signup, or the buy-credits flow. A new user who is interested in trying the product must notice the "Pricing" link in the nav bar -- the hero itself does not guide them there.

**P3 (Medium): Too many options on the pricing page for a first-time visitor.**
The pricing page presents 8 cards across two sections. For a brand-new user who just wants to try the product, the decision tree is overwhelming. The EAP card is positioned as one of four equally weighted "Free" options alongside Self-Hosted, Your Cloud, and White Label. A new user does not know which one they want. The "Cloud Shared" card (the Starter Pack) has an "Available Now" badge but is visually below the fold if the Free section cards are tall.

**P4 (Low): Second "Try SG/Send" CTA in the platform section repeats the same problem as P1.**
Line 100 of the home page has another `send.sgraph.ai` link. Same dead-end issue.

### Recommendations

| # | Priority | Recommendation |
|---|----------|---------------|
| R1 | Critical | Change the home page hero primary CTA from "Try SG/Send" (send.sgraph.ai) to "Get Started Free" linking to the Early Access page (`./early-access/`). Add "Try SG/Send" as a secondary CTA for users who already have a token. |
| R2 | High | Add a third hero CTA or a sub-hero banner on the home page: "Starting at GBP 5 for 500 files" linking to `./pricing/`. This gives price-aware users a direct path. |
| R3 | High | On the pricing page, visually distinguish the two recommended actions (EAP and Cloud Shared) from the other options. Consider: (a) a "Recommended for new users" section above the grid with just these two cards, or (b) adding a featured badge to the EAP card similar to the "Available Now" badge on Cloud Shared. |
| R4 | Medium | Add a "Don't have a token yet?" prompt to the send.sgraph.ai app itself (not in scope for this review but flagged here). The app should detect the absence of a token and display a link back to the EAP or pricing page. |

## 2. Language and Messaging

### What Works

- **"We literally cannot read your files"** -- this callout on the home page is the single best piece of copy across all pages. It translates a complex technical property into an emotionally compelling statement. It should be used more prominently.
- **"Files are encrypted before they ever leave your device"** -- another excellent plain-language explanation.
- **Pricing copy is transparent** -- "GBP 0.01 per file", "No payment required", "No credit card needed" are all clear and trust-building.

### Problems

**P5 (High): "Zero-knowledge encryption" is never defined for non-technical users.**
The term appears on every page (home page hero, pricing hero, encryption callout, feature cards, EAP page, Cloud page). It is used as a headline phrase, but no page ever explains what it means in practical terms. A business user -- say, a solicitor or HR director who needs to send sensitive documents -- will not know what "zero-knowledge" means. The closest explanation is "The server never sees your plaintext data, your file names, or your encryption keys" -- but this is buried in feature card body copy, not in the hero or callout where the term is most prominent.

**P6 (Medium): "AES-256-GCM" and "Web Crypto API" are used without context.**
These appear in the home page feature cards and the Cloud Shared page. They are meaningful to security engineers but noise to everyone else. They should be supplemented with a plain-language translation.

**P7 (Medium): Home page hero leads with the AI agent story, not the user problem.**
The headline is "AI agents that build, use, and secure the same platform." This is the story of how the product was built, not what it does for the user. A new visitor's first question is "What does this product do for me?" not "How was this product built?" The AI agent narrative is interesting but should be secondary.

**P8 (Low): "Serverless Pricing" card on the home page is confusing.**
The card says "Runs on AWS Lambda -- you only pay for what you use." A non-technical user reading this may think they need to set up AWS Lambda themselves, or may confuse this with the self-hosted deployment option. This card describes the SGraph cost structure, not a user benefit.

### Recommendations

| # | Priority | Recommendation |
|---|----------|---------------|
| R5 | High | Add a one-sentence plain-language definition of "zero-knowledge encryption" immediately after or below the first usage on each page. Suggested wording: "Zero-knowledge means that our servers never see your files, your file names, or your encryption keys. Only you and your recipient can access the content." This can be a subtitle under the hero or a tooltip/expandable note. |
| R6 | Medium | Replace or supplement "AES-256-GCM via the Web Crypto API" with: "Military-grade encryption built into your browser -- no software to install." Technical users can still find the specifics in a tooltip, footnote, or the Architecture page. |
| R7 | Medium | Rewrite the home page hero headline to lead with the user benefit. For example: "Send files that only your recipient can open." Move the AI agent story to the "Built in Public" section lower on the page. |
| R8 | Low | Rename the "Serverless Pricing" card to "Pay Only for What You Use" and rewrite the copy to focus on the user: "No monthly subscription. No minimum commitment. You pay per file transfer." Remove the AWS Lambda reference. |

## 3. EAP Signup Flow

### Current Flow

```
Pricing page "Join Early Access" --> early-access/index.html
                                      |
                                      v
                                    Form: Name + Email
                                      |
                                      v
                                    POST to getlaunchlist.com
                                      |
                                      v
                                    (Wait up to 48h for token via email)
                                      |
                                      v
                                    Go to send.sgraph.ai with token
```

### What Works

- **Minimal form fields** -- just name and email. Very low friction.
- **Clear expectation setting** -- "We'll send you an access token within 48 hours."
- **Benefits section** is well-laid-out and answers "What do I get?" before the user scrolls down.
- **"How it works" steps** on the EAP page are clear and numbered: sign up, get token, start sending, share securely.
- **Upgrade path is mentioned** -- "Upgrade anytime" and "Upgrade to the Starter Pack for just GBP 5."

### Problems

**P9 (High): No indication of how many evaluation credits the EAP provides.**
The card on the pricing page says "Limited evaluation credits" and the EAP page says "Free evaluation credits" and "Enough credits to fully evaluate the platform." But nowhere does it say how many credits. Is it 10 files? 50? 100? A user cannot make an informed decision to join vs. buy credits without knowing what "limited" means. This ambiguity may cause users to skip EAP entirely and go straight to the GBP 5 Starter Pack (not necessarily bad for revenue, but bad for conversion of users who are not yet ready to pay).

**P10 (Medium): No confirmation page or success state after form submission.**
The form POSTs to getlaunchlist.com. What happens after submission? Does the user see a confirmation? Are they redirected? There is no JavaScript handling of the form response in the page source. If getlaunchlist.com redirects to its own confirmation page, the user leaves the SG/Send site. If there is no feedback at all, the user does not know whether the submission worked.

**P11 (Medium): 48-hour wait may feel slow for an "early access" product.**
Modern SaaS users expect instant access. Waiting 48 hours for a token introduces a significant delay in the conversion funnel. Users who are evaluating multiple tools may lose interest.

**P12 (Low): The EAP page does not appear in the main nav.**
Users can only reach it via the pricing page EAP card or the "Why Free?" page CTA. If the EAP is the primary conversion goal, it should be more prominent.

### Recommendations

| # | Priority | Recommendation |
|---|----------|---------------|
| R9 | High | Specify the number of EAP credits on both the pricing page card and the EAP signup page. For example: "50 free evaluation credits" or whatever the actual number is. If the number varies, say "Up to X credits." |
| R10 | Medium | Add a client-side form submission handler that displays a confirmation message ("Check your inbox -- we'll send your token within 48 hours") without leaving the page. This provides immediate feedback and keeps the user on-site. |
| R11 | Medium | Consider reducing the token delivery time or implementing instant token issuance for EAP users. If manual review is required, state why: "We review each request to ensure quality -- your token will be delivered within 48 hours." This reframes the wait as a feature (quality control) rather than a limitation. |
| R12 | Low | Add "Early Access" as a visible CTA button in the nav bar (not a nav link, but a styled button) to increase discoverability. Many SaaS sites use this pattern for their primary conversion action. |

## 4. Buy Credits Flow

### Current Flow

```
Pricing page "Buy Credits" --> cloud/index.html
                                |
                                v
                              Cloud Shared detail page
                              (What's Included, How Credits Work)
                                |
                                v
                              "Buy Credits -- GBP 5" button
                                |
                                v
                              mailto:sherpa@sgraph.ai (!)
```

### What Works

- **Pricing is crystal clear** -- GBP 5 for 500 file transfers (GBP 0.01 per file) is stated multiple times and is easy to understand.
- **"What's Included" section** comprehensively covers infrastructure, encryption, pricing, credit expiry, payment, and support.
- **"How Credits Work" section** is a clean 3-step explanation (buy via Stripe, each upload uses one credit, credits valid for 3 months).
- **The 3-month expiry is clearly disclosed** -- no hidden gotchas.

### Problems

**P13 (Critical): The "Buy Credits" CTA is a mailto link, not a Stripe checkout.**
The page describes payment via Stripe extensively ("Stripe payment (card, Apple Pay)", "Payment is processed securely by Stripe", "Secure payment via Stripe"). But the actual "Buy Credits -- GBP 5" button at the bottom of the Cloud Shared page links to `mailto:sherpa@sgraph.ai?subject=Buy%20Credits...`. This is a fundamental disconnect. The user expects an instant checkout (as described) and instead gets an email composition window. This will cause immediate drop-off for users who expect a self-service purchase flow.

**P14 (High): No Stripe checkout link exists anywhere on the site.**
If Stripe checkout is not yet implemented, the copy should not promise it. The current state creates a trust gap -- the page says "Stripe payment" but delivers "email us."

**P15 (Medium): No mention of what happens when credits run out.**
The page explains credit expiry (3 months) but not what happens when you use all 500 credits before the 3 months are up. Can you buy another pack? Is there a top-up mechanism? Will you be notified when credits are running low?

### Recommendations

| # | Priority | Recommendation |
|---|----------|---------------|
| R13 | Critical | Either (a) implement the Stripe checkout link and use it as the CTA, or (b) rewrite the copy to be honest about the current process: "Email us to purchase -- we'll set up your credits and send your token." Do not describe Stripe checkout if the user cannot self-serve through it. |
| R14 | High | If Stripe is not yet ready, add a clear transition message: "We're currently onboarding customers manually to ensure the best experience. Email sherpa@sgraph.ai and we'll have you set up within 24 hours." This reframes the manual process positively. |
| R15 | Medium | Add a "What happens when my credits run out?" section to both the Cloud Shared page and the Pricing FAQ. Suggested answer: "Simply buy another Starter Pack. There is no subscription -- you only pay when you need more credits." |

## 5. FAQ Effectiveness

### Current FAQ Questions (Pricing Page)

1. What counts as one file transfer?
2. Is the free tier really free?
3. What's the difference between shared and dedicated?
4. Can I switch between deployment modes?
5. Who operates SG/Send?

### Assessment

The FAQ answers questions that a mid-funnel user (someone already considering options) would ask. It does not answer the questions a top-of-funnel user (someone who just arrived) would ask.

### Missing Questions a New User Would Ask

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| "Is my data safe?" / "Can you see my files?" | The number one concern for anyone sending sensitive files. The encryption callout addresses this but it is not in the FAQ. |
| "How big can the files be?" | File size limits are critical for business users. Not mentioned anywhere. |
| "How long are files stored?" | Retention policy is not disclosed anywhere. |
| "What happens when my credits run out?" | See P15. |
| "Can I try it before I pay?" | The EAP answers this, but the FAQ does not mention it. |
| "Do I need to install anything?" | Answered briefly in "How it works" but not in the FAQ. |
| "What file types are supported?" | Business users will want to know if they can send PDFs, spreadsheets, etc. |
| "Can I cancel or get a refund?" | Standard purchase concern, especially for a new/unknown service. |

### Recommendations

| # | Priority | Recommendation |
|---|----------|---------------|
| R16 | High | Add these FAQ entries: "Is my data safe?", "Can I try before I pay?", "How big can files be?", "What happens when credits run out?" |
| R17 | Medium | Add these FAQ entries: "How long are files stored?", "Do I need to install anything?", "What file types can I send?" |
| R18 | Low | Add: "Can I get a refund?" and "What happens if I do not use all my credits before they expire?" |

## 6. Support Accessibility

### What Works

- **sherpa@sgraph.ai appears on every page footer** -- both as a link in the "Connect" column and as a styled CTA button ("Got a question? Email the Sherpa").
- **The footer CTA button is visually distinct** -- it uses `btn-sherpa` styling, which should make it stand out.
- **The Cloud Shared page lists "Email support via Sherpa"** as an included feature.
- **The Early Access page mentions "Our Sherpa will send you an access token within 48 hours"** -- the Sherpa is named as the point of contact.

### Problems

**P16 (Medium): No other support channel besides email.**
There is no chat widget, no knowledge base / help centre, no FAQ on the EAP page, and no community forum. Email-only support is acceptable for early stage, but should be acknowledged.

**P17 (Low): The Sherpa is not explained on the pricing or cloud pages.**
On the home page, the Sherpa is described as an AI agent role ("Guides users through SG/Send and answers questions"). On the pricing and cloud pages, "Sherpa" is used as if the user already knows what it means. A new user may not know whether "Sherpa" is a team, a person, a chatbot, or a brand name.

### Recommendations

| # | Priority | Recommendation |
|---|----------|---------------|
| R19 | Medium | Add a brief note near the first mention of "Sherpa" on the pricing and cloud pages: "The Sherpa is our user support team -- email sherpa@sgraph.ai and a real person will respond." |
| R20 | Low | Consider adding a simple FAQ or help section to the EAP page answering "What if I have a problem?" with the sherpa@sgraph.ai contact. |

## 7. Common User Objections

### Objection: "Is my data safe?"

**Addressed?** Partially. The encryption callout ("We literally cannot read your files") is excellent but only appears on the home page. The pricing page has a similar callout ("Every mode uses the same zero-knowledge encryption... We never see your data") but it is positioned between the Free and Cloud sections, not near the conversion CTAs. A user who scrolls quickly to the CTA buttons may miss it.

**Recommendation (R21):** Repeat the "We literally cannot read your files" callout directly above or below the EAP and Cloud Shared CTA buttons. Place the strongest trust signal closest to the conversion action.

### Objection: "What happens when credits run out?"

**Addressed?** No. Not mentioned anywhere. See P15 and R15.

### Objection: "Can I try before I pay?"

**Addressed?** Yes, but indirectly. The EAP card is positioned in the "Free" section and says "No payment required." However, the two conversion paths (EAP and Buy Credits) are separated by a large section of the pricing page. A user reading the Cloud Shared card may not realise there is a free trial option above. The FAQ does not mention the EAP as an answer to "Can I try it?"

**Recommendation (R22):** Add a line to the Cloud Shared card or the Cloud Shared page: "Not ready to buy? Try SG/Send free with the Early Access Programme" with a link to the EAP page.

### Objection: "I have never heard of SGraph AI / The Cyber Boardroom. Can I trust them?"

**Addressed?** Partially. The FAQ mentions "The Cyber Boardroom Ltd, a company registered in England and Wales." The GitHub link provides transparency. The "Built in Public" section with stats (393 passing tests, 73 endpoints) adds credibility. However, there are no customer testimonials, case studies, security certifications, or third-party endorsements.

**Recommendation (R23):** For the current stage (early access), this is acceptable. But flag for the Ambassador/Journalist: as the product matures, add a "Trusted by" section or security audit badge near the conversion CTAs.

### Objection: "GBP 5 seems cheap -- is this a real product?"

**Addressed?** No. Paradoxically, the very low price may cause credibility concerns. Some users may wonder why it is so cheap and whether the service is reliable.

**Recommendation (R24):** Add a line to the Cloud Shared page or pricing FAQ explaining the pricing rationale: "Our serverless architecture means we only pay for what you use. We pass those savings on to you. No idle servers = no inflated pricing."

### Objection: "I need this for my company. Is there a business/enterprise option?"

**Addressed?** Yes. The Dedicated and Managed options are clearly presented on the pricing page with "Contact Us" CTAs.
