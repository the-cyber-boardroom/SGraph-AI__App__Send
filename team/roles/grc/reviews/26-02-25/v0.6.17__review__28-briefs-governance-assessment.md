## Brief #24: Signed Creative Commons — Legal Enforcement (STRATEGIC, Lead)

The CC-Signed proposal extends CC licences with cryptographic signature preservation requirements. This is the most consequential GRC brief in the batch.

**Strengths:** Decades of CC case law, digital signatures have legal standing (eIDAS, ESIGN Act, UETA), maps to existing enforcement mechanisms.

**Risks:** No precedent for signature preservation as a licence condition. CC Foundation engagement required before claiming "CC-Signed" branding. eIDAS "advanced electronic signature" classification must be validated. LLM training data obligations under CC BY-S are untested.

## Brief #2: Trust Web — Privacy Implications (CRITICAL, Supporting)

The Trust Web requires the server to know communication patterns (which public keys interact). This metadata exposure must be documented under GDPR.

**Key findings:**
- IP hash + public key hash + timestamps constitutes personal data if individual is identifiable
- Daily-salt SHA-256 IP hashing provides pseudonymisation, not anonymisation
- Right to erasure (Art. 17) conflicts with trust score persistence

**GRC requirements:** (1) DPIA required before deployment. (2) Privacy notice must disclose metadata collection. (3) Lawful basis: legitimate interest for fraud prevention (Art. 6(1)(f)). (4) Trust-level gating (5 files/day for anonymous) must be assessed for discrimination and proportionality under competition law.

## Brief #8: Network Threat Detection — Policy Implications (HIGH, Supporting)

**Silent surveillance:** Monitoring without notification has GDPR implications. Fraud prevention legitimate interest applies but proportionality must be demonstrated. Retention periods for surveillance data must be defined.

**Cascade destruction:** Collective sanctions (destroying associated accounts) require an appeals process. Each cascade decision must be logged with justification.

**Law enforcement:** Pattern extraction may constitute criminal evidence. A law enforcement request protocol must be defined before this capability goes live.

## Brief #13: Fractal Document Signing — Contract/EULA Implications (STRATEGIC, Supporting)

Per-clause signing has legal standing under eIDAS and ESIGN/UETA. The .sgdoc format must meet "reliable electronic signature" requirements per jurisdiction.

**EULA third-party review model risks:** Reviewer liability if an approved clause proves harmful. Consumer reliance on "reviewed by Privacy Foundation" badges may be actionable. Micropayment model for clause review must comply with financial services regulation.

**Recommendation:** Engage legal counsel on third-party reviewer liability framework before feature ships.

---

## Brief #22: Agent Isolation — Security Claims Validation (CRITICAL, Supporting)

The architecture correction (encryption alone is not isolation) has governance implications: any previous "cryptographic isolation" claims must be corrected in marketing and investor materials. SOC 2/ISO 27001 audit scope would include container isolation controls.

Container isolation with ephemeral environments supports GDPR data minimisation (Art. 5(1)(c)). Private key injection via secret management must be documented in the processing record.

**GRC validation accepted:** Will review container isolation against SOC 2 CC6.1 (logical access) and CC6.6 (system boundary).
