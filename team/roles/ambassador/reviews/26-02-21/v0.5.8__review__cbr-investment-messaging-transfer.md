## 3. Communication Patterns Worth Adopting

### 3.1 The "Hostile FAQ" Pattern

CBR's engineering documents include a "Hostile FAQ" section -- pre-emptive answers to skeptical questions. This is excellent investor preparation.

**Recommended SG/Send hostile FAQ topics:**
- "Isn't this just another WeTransfer clone?"
- "If encryption is client-side, how do you add features?"
- "What happens if someone loses their decryption key?"
- "How do you compete with Signal's file sharing?"
- "Isn't zero-knowledge encryption a liability if law enforcement needs access?"
- "One person and AI agents? How do you scale?"

### 3.2 The Minerva Pattern (AI-Assisted Investor Communication)

CBR has a dedicated AI persona (Minerva) for investor communication. SG/Send's Alchemist role is the human equivalent. Consider whether a SG/Send-specific investor chatbot could be useful for:
- Answering common investor questions from source materials
- Generating personalised investor briefings
- Demo companion during investor meetings

### 3.3 The "Billable Units" Language

CBR's financial documents use "billable units" -- every feature must create a measurable unit of value. This language should be adopted for SG/Send:
- Transfer = billable unit (per-transfer pricing)
- Storage-day = billable unit (per-day storage pricing)
- PKI key exchange = billable unit (per-authentication pricing)
- Deployment instance = billable unit (per-deployment licensing)
