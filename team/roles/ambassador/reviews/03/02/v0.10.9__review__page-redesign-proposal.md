## Executive Summary

The redesign proposal is directionally excellent. It addresses every structural deficiency that the Ambassador identified in the UX brief. The core move -- "Buy Credits" as primary CTA linking directly to Stripe, one click from the homepage hero -- is the single most important change for Campaign success. However, there are eight areas where the proposal needs refinement to maximise campaign ROI, ensure post-conversion success, and avoid creating new problems while solving old ones.

**Overall verdict:** APPROVE with modifications. The P0 items should ship immediately. The modifications below should be incorporated into P1/P2 work.

## 1. Challenge Campaign Impact

### Assessment: Strong, with one gap

The proposed homepage hero delivers exactly what the Challenge needs. A participant arriving at sgraph.ai after receiving the WhatsApp message will see:

```
Send files. Nobody can read them. Not even us.
[Buy Credits -- GBP 5 ->]   [Try Free ->]
```

This is a direct match to the Challenge instruction ("go to sgraph.ai and buy GBP 5 of credits"). The participant sees the price, sees the action, and can act in one click. This is transformational compared to the current 4-click, 4-scroll path.

**The gap:** The proposal does not address what happens between Stripe checkout completion and the user actually sending their first encrypted file. The Challenge requires the participant to complete the full journey: buy -> get token -> send file -> recipient opens. The current `/payment/success/` page says:

> "We'll create your access token (typically within a few hours)"

This is a conversion cliff. The participant has just paid, has momentum, and is told to wait "a few hours." For the Challenge, this delay will kill completion rates. The Ambassador's recommendation is:

**Recommendation 1:** The `/payment/success/` page must include a clear timeline and set expectations that align with the Challenge pace. If token provisioning cannot be made instant, the success page should provide an interim activity ("Bookmark send.sgraph.ai -- you'll receive your token within [X] hours") and a reason to stay engaged ("While you wait, here's how the encryption works"). Better still: automate token provisioning so it arrives within minutes, not hours. The Challenge's value depends on same-session completion.

**Recommendation 2:** Consider whether the Stripe Payment Link can be configured to include a `client_reference_id` or `metadata` field that flags this as a Challenge purchase, enabling faster/prioritised token provisioning for Challenge participants.

## 2. Conversion Funnel Analysis

### Current state: Buy path

```
Homepage -> "See Pricing" -> /pricing/ -> scroll -> scroll -> "Buy Credits" -> /pricing/cloud/ -> scroll -> "Buy Credits -- GBP 5" -> Stripe
Steps: 4 clicks, 4 scrolls, 4 page loads
```

Applying the industry benchmark of ~20% drop-off per additional step:

| Step | Remaining | Calculation |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Land on homepage | 100% | -- |
| Click "See Pricing" | 80% | -20% |
| Scroll past philosophy | 64% | -20% |
| Find and click "Buy Credits" on card | 51% | -20% |
| Scroll past "What's Included" on /pricing/cloud/ | 41% | -20% |
| Click "Buy Credits -- GBP 5" | 33% | -20% |
| Complete Stripe checkout | 26% | -20% (Stripe's own drop-off) |

**Current estimated conversion: ~26% of intentful visitors complete purchase.**

### Proposed state: Buy path

```
Homepage -> "Buy Credits -- GBP 5" -> Stripe
Steps: 1 click, 0 scrolls, 1 page load
```

| Step | Remaining | Calculation |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Land on homepage | 100% | -- |
| Click "Buy Credits -- GBP 5" | 80% | -20% |
| Complete Stripe checkout | 64% | -20% (Stripe's own drop-off) |

**Proposed estimated conversion: ~64% of intentful visitors complete purchase.**

### Projected lift: 2.5x improvement (26% -> 64%)

For the Challenge campaign specifically, the audience is highly intentful (they have been personally asked by a friend and offered a GBP 5 Amazon voucher for completion). The drop-off rates will likely be lower than the 20% industry average for this primed audience, potentially reaching 70-75% completion.

### Current state: Free path

```
Homepage -> "Get Started Free" -> /pricing/free/ -> scroll -> "Join Early Access" -> /early-access/ -> fill form -> LaunchList redirect
Steps: 3 clicks, 1 scroll, 3 page loads, 1 brand break
```

Estimated conversion with brand break penalty (~40% additional drop-off at LaunchList): ~25%

### Proposed state: Free path

```
Homepage -> "Try Free" -> scroll to inline form -> fill -> inline confirmation
Steps: 0 clicks (1 scroll), 0 page loads, 0 brand breaks
```

Estimated conversion: ~75% (single scroll + inline form has minimal friction)

**Free path lift: 3x improvement (25% -> 75%)**

### Ambassador's verdict on funnel

The funnel improvements are substantial and well-grounded. The proposal correctly identifies that every intermediate page is pure friction for the Challenge audience. The Ambassador endorses the funnel restructuring without reservation.

## 3. Messaging and Copy

### "Buy Credits -- GBP 5 ->" as primary CTA

**Assessment: Good but not optimal.**

Strengths:
- Includes the price (GBP 5) -- removes uncertainty about what "Buy Credits" costs
- Action-oriented verb ("Buy")
- Arrow indicates progression

Weaknesses:
- "Credits" is an internal concept the user has not yet learned. A Challenge participant was told "buy GBP 5 of credits" by their friend, so the word "credits" has context in that case. But for organic visitors, "credits" is ambiguous -- credits for what? How many? What can I do with them?
- "Buy" is transactional and slightly cold for a first interaction

**Alternative CTAs to test (in priority order):**

| CTA | Rationale |
|-----|-----------|
| **"Get Started -- GBP 5 ->"** | Focuses on the outcome (getting started) rather than the transaction (buying). Still includes price. Less transactional friction. |
| **"Buy Credits -- GBP 5 ->"** (as proposed) | Direct, clear, matches the Challenge message phrasing. Works well for primed audiences. |
| **"Start Sending -- GBP 5 ->"** | Connects price to the core action. Implies immediate capability. |
| **"500 Encrypted Transfers -- GBP 5 ->"** | Leads with value quantity. Makes the economics obvious. |

**Recommendation 3:** For the Challenge launch, use "Buy Credits -- GBP 5 ->" because it matches the language in the WhatsApp message. After the Challenge, A/B test "Get Started -- GBP 5" against it. The Ambassador's hypothesis is that "Get Started" will outperform "Buy Credits" for organic traffic but underperform for primed Challenge traffic.

### "Try Free ->" as secondary CTA

**Assessment: Good.**

"Try Free" is clear, low-commitment, and accurately describes what happens. The arrow suggests forward motion. The word "Try" correctly sets the expectation that this is an evaluation, not a permanent free tier.

One concern: "Try Free" could be confused with a free trial that expires automatically. The inline form below clarifies this, but consider whether "Join Free ->" or "Get Free Access ->" would be clearer about what the user receives (an access token, not a time-limited trial).

### Sub-hero text

The proposed sub-hero text is:

> "Open source . GBP 0.01/file . 500 transfers"
> "Already have a token? Go to send.sgraph.ai ->"

**Assessment: Excellent.** The three bullet points communicate: philosophy (open source), economics (penny per file), and scale (500 transfers). The "Already have a token?" line correctly serves returning users without cluttering the primary CTA area.

## 4. Stripe Payment Link Strategy

### Current state

The Stripe Payment Link URL is: `https://buy.stripe.com/fZu8wHdv23nN1Z85jn5os02`

This URL appears exactly once in the current codebase -- on `/pricing/cloud/index.html`. The proposal would place this same URL on every "Buy Credits" button across the homepage and pricing page.

### UTM parameters

**Recommendation 4:** Every "Buy Credits" link should include UTM parameters for attribution. Stripe Payment Links support URL parameters that are passed through to the success/cancel redirect URLs.

Proposed parameter scheme:

| Page | URL |
|------|-----|
| Homepage hero | `?utm_source=website&utm_medium=hero&utm_campaign=challenge` |
| Homepage "Deploy Anywhere" section | `?utm_source=website&utm_medium=deploy_section&utm_campaign=challenge` |
| Pricing page (Starter Pack card) | `?utm_source=website&utm_medium=pricing_card&utm_campaign=challenge` |
| Pricing page (Multi-Tenant card) | `?utm_source=website&utm_medium=pricing_cloud&utm_campaign=challenge` |

This enables post-campaign analysis of which page placement drives the most conversions. Without UTM tracking, we will know people bought credits but not which button they clicked.

**Important caveat:** Stripe Payment Links accept `client_reference_id` as a query parameter. This can be used to pass a campaign identifier that appears in the Stripe dashboard and webhook events. This is more reliable than UTM parameters (which depend on the redirect URL parsing). The recommendation is to use both.

### Multiple Stripe links vs. one link

**Assessment: One link is correct.** Using a single Stripe Payment Link with UTM differentiation is the right approach. Multiple Stripe links for the same product create inventory management complexity and make it harder to update pricing. The UTM parameters provide the attribution we need.

### Success/cancel page messaging

**The `/payment/success/` page needs work.** Currently it says:

> "We'll create your access token (typically within a few hours)"

For the Challenge campaign, this page is the bridge between "I paid" and "I use the product." It must:

1. Confirm the payment clearly (already does this)
2. Set a specific timeline for token delivery (not "a few hours" -- give a number)
3. Provide the next action ("Check your email for a message from Sherpa")
4. Include a fallback ("Haven't received your token? Email sherpa@sgraph.ai")
5. Keep the user in the SG/Send ecosystem ("While you wait, learn how the encryption works")

**The `/payment/cancel/` page is adequate** but should include a direct re-try link back to Stripe (not just "Back to Pricing"). Currently it sends the user back to the pricing page, re-introducing the funnel they just navigated. Add a "Try again ->" button that goes directly to the Stripe Payment Link.

**Recommendation 5:** Redesign the `/payment/success/` page as a "Welcome" page, not just a receipt confirmation. This is the first moment the user is a customer -- treat it as onboarding, not as a transaction confirmation.

## 5. Post-Conversion Nurture

### The gap between "I paid" and "I'm a happy user"

The proposal focuses entirely on the pre-conversion funnel (homepage -> Stripe) and says nothing about the post-conversion experience. This is a significant gap for the Challenge campaign, because the Challenge requires the participant to complete the full journey, not just buy credits.

**Current post-conversion flow:**

```
Stripe checkout -> /payment/success/ -> "wait a few hours" -> email from Sherpa -> send.sgraph.ai -> upload file -> share link -> recipient opens
```

**Problems:**

1. **Token delay** -- "Typically within a few hours" is manual provisioning. For the Challenge, we are sending 50-100 people through this funnel in a concentrated period. If token provisioning is manual, the Sherpa will be overwhelmed and the delay will grow.

2. **No bridging content** -- After paying, the user has nothing to do until their token arrives. No tutorial, no demo, no preview. Momentum dies.

3. **No progress tracking** -- The Challenge requires confirmation that each participant completed the full flow. There is no mechanism for tracking whether a buyer actually sent a file and had it opened by a recipient.

**Recommendation 6:** For the Challenge specifically, create a dedicated post-purchase nurture sequence:

| Touchpoint | Timing | Content |
|------------|--------|---------|
| `/payment/success/` page | Immediate | "Your token is being created. Check your inbox within [X] hours." + link to "How it works" video/guide |
| Token delivery email | Within 2 hours (target 30 mins) | Token + step-by-step instructions + "Send your first file now" CTA |
| Follow-up email | 24h after token delivery (if no upload detected) | "You bought credits but haven't sent a file yet. Here's how to get started." |
| Challenge completion confirmation | When recipient opens file | "Challenge complete! Here's your Amazon voucher code." (or manual follow-up from Dinis) |

The Ambassador acknowledges that steps 3 and 4 require infrastructure that may not exist yet. At minimum, the success page and token delivery email should be optimised before Challenge launch.

## 6. SEO and External Link Risks

### Pages proposed for elimination

| Page | Canonical URL | In sitemap? | Hreflang variants? | Internal links from |
|------|--------------|-------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| `/pricing/cloud/` | Yes | Yes (all 16 locales) | Yes (16 locales) | Homepage "Buy Credits", Pricing page "Buy Credits" card, `/pricing/free/` CTA |
| `/pricing/free/` | Yes | Yes (all 16 locales) | Yes (16 locales) | Homepage "Get Started Free", Pricing page "Why is it free?" link |
| `/early-access/` | Yes | Yes (all 16 locales) | Yes (16 locales) | Pricing page "Join Early Access" button, `/pricing/free/` CTA |

### Risk assessment

**`/pricing/cloud/`** -- This page has a well-optimised meta description ("SG/Send Cloud shared hosting -- GBP 5 for 500 encrypted file transfers. Zero-knowledge encryption on shared multi-tenant infrastructure.") and structured breadcrumb data. If Google has indexed this page and it ranks for any long-tail queries (e.g., "encrypted file sharing GBP 5", "zero knowledge file transfer pricing"), eliminating it without a redirect will cause a 404 and lose that traffic.

**`/pricing/free/`** -- Similarly indexed with strong SEO metadata. The title "Why Free?" is a potential search intent match for users researching free encrypted file sharing options.

**`/early-access/`** -- Indexed across 16 locales. The sitemap explicitly lists it. If any social media posts, LinkedIn messages, or previous outreach have linked to this URL, those links will break.

### Mitigation strategy

**Recommendation 7:** Do NOT simply delete these pages. Implement 301 redirects:

| Old URL | Redirect to | Rationale |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| `/pricing/cloud/` | `/pricing/#cloud` (anchor on pricing page) | Preserves SEO equity. Users land on the pricing page scrolled to the cloud section. |
| `/pricing/free/` | `/pricing/#free` (anchor on pricing page) | Preserves SEO equity. Matches the proposal's own suggestion. |
| `/early-access/` | `/#try-free` (anchor on homepage inline form) | Preserves any external links. User lands on the homepage scrolled to the signup form. |

Additionally:
- Update the sitemap to remove the old URLs and add the new anchor targets
- Update all hreflang tags on remaining pages to remove references to eliminated pages
- Monitor Google Search Console for 404 errors in the two weeks following the change
- Keep the old pages as redirect stubs for at least 6 months before full removal

**External link audit needed:** Before eliminating pages, search for external references:
- LinkedIn posts by Dinis or SGraph AI accounts
- Any WhatsApp messages already sent with sgraph.ai links
- GitHub README or documentation links
- Any press mentions or blog posts
- LaunchList referral URLs (these go to `send.sgraph.ai/?ref=...`, not the pages being eliminated, so these are safe)

The proposal correctly notes that `/early-access/` has "no external links to it." This needs verification. The sitemap's inclusion across 16 locales suggests it was intended to be discoverable. The Ambassador recommends treating the assertion as unverified until confirmed.

## 8. Competitive Positioning

### Is price-leading the right strategy?

The proposed homepage leads with "Buy Credits -- GBP 5" as the primary CTA. This positions SG/Send as a price-first product. For a security product, this is a deliberate positioning choice with trade-offs.

**Arguments for price-leading:**

1. **GBP 5 is disarmingly low.** For the Challenge audience (friends and contacts), GBP 5 is trivial -- less than a coffee. The low price removes the objection "I'm not sure I want to spend money on this." It reframes the purchase as a favour, not a financial decision.

2. **Transparency signals trust.** Showing the price upfront, with no hidden tiers or enterprise-contact-us gates, communicates the same honesty that the zero-knowledge encryption communicates technically. "We tell you exactly what it costs" is aligned with "We tell you exactly what we can see (nothing)."

3. **It differentiates from competitors.** Most encrypted file sharing products (Tresorit, SpiderOak, etc.) hide their pricing behind enterprise sales flows or require account creation before showing a price. SG/Send showing "GBP 5" in the hero is distinctive.

**Arguments against price-leading:**

1. **Security products should lead with trust, not price.** When someone is choosing an encryption product, their primary concern is "can I trust this?" not "how much does it cost?" Leading with price could signal that SG/Send competes on cost rather than security. The wrong inference is: "it's cheap, so maybe the encryption is cheap too."

2. **GBP 5 could signal "not serious" to enterprise evaluators.** A CISO evaluating encrypted file transfer for their organisation might see "GBP 5" and dismiss it as a consumer toy. The pricing page's deeper options (single-tenant, managed) serve this audience, but they will never scroll that far if the hero signals "consumer product."

3. **Price anchoring.** Once you lead with GBP 5, every subsequent pricing conversation is anchored there. If SG/Send later introduces enterprise pricing at GBP 500/month, the GBP 5 anchor makes that seem expensive.

### Ambassador's position

**For the Challenge campaign specifically, price-leading is correct.** The Challenge audience is pre-qualified (friends and contacts), pre-informed (they have been told what to do), and pre-motivated (Amazon voucher). For this audience, the price is not a trust signal -- it is a logistics detail. Making it visible and actionable is the right move.

**For long-term organic positioning, reconsider.** After the Challenge, the Ambassador recommends testing a hero that leads with the security proposition and places the price in the CTA itself rather than as the CTA's sole message:

```
Current proposal:    [Buy Credits -- GBP 5 ->]
Alternative:         [Get Started -- GBP 5 ->]
Long-term test:      [Start Sending Encrypted Files ->]  (price shown below the button as a sub-label)
```

The principle: the CTA communicates what the user will be able to do (send encrypted files), and the price is supporting information rather than the primary message.

**Recommendation 9:** Accept the price-leading approach for the Challenge launch. Schedule a positioning review after the Challenge concludes, using conversion data and qualitative feedback to decide whether to shift to trust-leading for organic traffic.
