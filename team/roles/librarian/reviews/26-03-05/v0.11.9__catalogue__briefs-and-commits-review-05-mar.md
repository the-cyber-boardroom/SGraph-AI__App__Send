## Observations

### Brief Volume Is Accelerating

12 briefs in a single day (4 March) is the highest volume yet. The briefs span from immediate dev work (HTML Workbench) to long-term architecture (Git-like object store) to business strategy (DPO, healthcare, custody services). The human is thinking at multiple time horizons simultaneously.

### The Workspace Is the Active Frontier

Of the 12 code-bearing commits since the last catalogue, **9 are workspace enhancements**. The workspace has gone from "first LLM call" to a functioning HTML transformation workbench in less than 48 hours. This is the fastest-moving area of the codebase.

### Business Briefs Are Investor/Meeting Preparation

Five of the 12 briefs (DPO, GDPR, healthcare, custody, use-case viewers) are business/strategy briefs with clear meeting preparation or investor narrative purposes. They map existing capabilities to specific market opportunities. The DPO brief explicitly notes a meeting is imminent.

### Stripe Automation Remains Overdue

The daily brief flags Stripe automation as P0 for the third consecutive mention. No brief, design, or implementation exists. This is the highest-priority gap in the operational infrastructure.

### Architecture Vision Is Expanding Faster Than Implementation

The Git-like object store, Chrome extension, JavaScript pipeline, hash caching, and privacy-preserving content assembly represent months of potential work. None have started implementation. The gap between vision and code is growing. This is expected in the Explorer phase — briefs capture ideas while they are fresh — but the Librarian notes it for prioritisation awareness.
