## Executive Summary

The sgraph.ai website must be redesigned to reflect what v0.3.0 actually is: not just "secure file transfer", but **encrypted file sharing where recipients browse, not just download**. The current site fails on three counts: it shows no product, it ignores its highest-volume visitor (the recipient), and it positions a secondary story (AI-built) ahead of its primary differentiator (gallery/folder/SgPrint viewing experience).

This brief defines what to build, in what order, and why. The Developer implementation map (companion document) defines how.

## 3. The Six Non-Negotiables

1. **Six-sentence privacy policy displayed visibly on the homepage** — not linked, not referenced, the full six sentences in the page body. "6 sentences vs their 4,000 words."

2. **No product screenshots = no homepage** — the current site has zero. Every major section needs real, annotated product screenshots. No mockups.

3. **`<sg-public-viewer>` in the hero** — live encrypted content in the right column of the hero. Graceful fallback to static screenshot. This is the single highest-impact design decision.

4. **Recipient journey designed, not accidental** — the path from "I received a file" → "I now want to send one" must be a deliberate, designed experience.

5. **Stats bar removed** — "18 AI Agents / 73 Endpoints / 393 Tests / 1 Human" off the homepage entirely. Test counts are inaccurate (tests span multiple repos, no single number is meaningful without context) and the raw number means nothing to visitors. Replace with: "Open source — auditable on GitHub" + link. Test stats will be reintroduced when we have a docs page that maps all repos and their testing status.

6. **`/security/` deployed BEFORE `/architecture/` is removed** — to avoid ranking gap. 301 redirect only after the new page is indexed.

## 6. Homepage Copy (Final)

### Hero
**H1:** "Share files. Browse them. Nobody can read them. Not even us."
**Sub:** "Files are encrypted in your browser before upload. Recipients get a gallery, a folder, or a PDF — without downloading. The server never sees your data."
**Primary CTA:** "Try Free — No Account Required →"
**Secondary CTA:** "Buy Credits — £5 →"

### How It Works (3 steps)
1. **Drop** — Add files. Choose your share mode.
2. **Encrypt** — AES-256-GCM runs in your browser. The key never leaves your device.
3. **Share** — Send a friendly token. Recipients browse, view, and interact — no download required.

### Trust Callout
> "Zero cookies. Zero tracking. We cannot read your files."
> "Privacy policy: 6 sentences. Theirs: 4,000 words."

### Six-Sentence Policy (displayed in full)
> 1. We encrypt your files in your browser before upload.
> 2. We cannot decrypt your files because we never have the key.
> 3. We do not use cookies, tracking pixels, or analytics on the product.
> 4. We store only encrypted ciphertext and a hashed IP address.
> 5. We delete your data after the retention period you chose.
> 6. That's everything. There is nothing else.

### Primary Conversion CTA (below share modes)
**"Try it now. No signup required."**
[Try Free →]  [Buy Credits — £5 →]

## 8. Competitive Positioning (On-Site)

### Comparison table for `/features/` or `/security/`

| Feature | SG/Send | WeTransfer | Proton Drive |
|---------|---------|------------|--------------|
| Zero-knowledge (client-side) | ✅ | ❌ server-side | ✅ client-side |
| Recipients browse without downloading | ✅ gallery + folder | ❌ download only | ❌ download only |
| No account required (sender or recipient) | ✅ token-based | ❌ email required | ❌ account required |
| Friendly tokens | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ |
| Zero cookies (verifiable in DevTools) | ✅ | ❌ | ❌ |
| Pay-per-use (no subscription) | ✅ £0.01/file | ❌ subscription | ❌ subscription |
| Privacy policy | 6 sentences | 4,000+ words | Long |
| Open source | ✅ | ❌ | Partial |

*Note: Proton Drive also uses client-side E2EE — encryption is not the differentiator vs Proton. Our edge is the recipient browsing experience, no-account model, and pay-per-use pricing.*

### WeTransfer positioning
"We can't train AI on files we can't see." — use this on the homepage or /security/ page. It's factually accurate and differentiating.

## 12. What This Is NOT

To avoid scope creep:

- ❌ Not a full rebrand — Aurora theme stays, tokens stay
- ❌ Not a new CMS — stays static HTML/CSS/JS on S3/CloudFront
- ❌ Not a translation project — en-gb and en-us first for new pages
- ❌ Not a tools.sgraph.ai build — use `sgraph.ai/tools/` path when ready, but speed test is out of scope for this sprint
- ❌ Not a `/investors/` page — investor audience served by homepage + existing pitch materials
- ❌ Not a blog/CMS — no new infrastructure

## 13. QA Site Integration

`qa.send.sgraph.ai` already contains a wealth of content that the main website should actively reference and link to. This is not duplication — it is the depth layer behind the marketing layer.

| QA site content | How sgraph.ai should use it |
|----------------|----------------------------|
| Test report pages with screenshots | Link from /features/ — "See it tested →" |
| Architecture documentation | Source material for /security/ page |
| Use case test pages | Link from /why/ pages — "See it in action →" |
| Agent team pages | Footer link "Meet the team building this →" |
| Vault browser tool (from tools.sgraph.ai) | Link from /features/ and /security/ |

**Key principle:** The QA site is the proof layer. sgraph.ai is the pitch layer. Every claim on sgraph.ai should have a QA site link as the "verify this" destination.
