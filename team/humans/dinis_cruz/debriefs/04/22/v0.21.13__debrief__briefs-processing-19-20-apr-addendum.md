## Executive Summary

The April 19–20 briefs are the most strategically dense pair of days since the product launched. Two clusters:

**April 19 — Tools Ecosystem Plumbing:**
The missing composability layer for every tool on the platform. The send/receive JS API (`SgSend.sendFile()`, web components) means any tool can save and share output with a single call. Video editing gets three new capabilities (dynamic crop for redacting screen recordings, multi-track overlay, stream capture). The QA architecture gets a full overhaul: three Playwright modes, a single abstraction layer, and an hourly synthetic traffic heartbeat as the observability foundation.

**April 20 — Strategy and Positioning:**
The most significant day since launch. The hero positioning line is decided and committed: "Version-controlled, client-encrypted vaults. For humans and agents." The three agentic primitives are articulated (browser automation with MITM, encrypted VFS, code execution sandbox). A three-AMI AWS Marketplace strategy packages these as products. A website redesign with a hard Wednesday deadline (today) applies the positioning. A personalised outreach workflow turns every AWS Conference follow-up into a product demo.

**One critical defect noted in the briefs: the receive flow on sgraph.ai is broken.** Visitors with a token cannot easily find where to paste it. This must be fixed immediately — it's the single biggest demo-blocker.
