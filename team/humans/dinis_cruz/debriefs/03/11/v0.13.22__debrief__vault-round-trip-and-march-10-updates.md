## Executive Summary

March 10 was a landmark day for SG/Send. The headline achievement: **Claude successfully used `sg-send-cli` to read and write to a zero-knowledge encrypted vault, completing a full bidirectional round-trip with a human developer**. A second Claude session then bootstrapped itself from `SKILL.md` in the vault — zero human instruction required.

Alongside the vault breakthrough, the team produced investor materials (Alchemist track), a deploy service architecture (Deploy team), and multiple CLI architecture decisions (CLI team). The version jumped 23 minor versions in one day.

---

## Headline: The Vault Round-Trip

**Source:** [v0.13.31 Round-Trip Debrief](../../../briefs/03/10/v0.13.31__round-trip-debrief.md)

The round-trip proved five things:
1. **Claude can be a vault peer** — clone, read, write, push via `sg-send-cli`
2. **The vault is a communication channel** — async, persistent, bidirectional, encrypted
3. **Self-bootstrapping works** — SKILL.md in the vault lets new sessions operate autonomously
4. **Zero-knowledge holds end-to-end** — server never sees plaintext, even with AI participants
5. **Agent-to-agent communication is now possible** — multiple Claude sessions sharing a vault
