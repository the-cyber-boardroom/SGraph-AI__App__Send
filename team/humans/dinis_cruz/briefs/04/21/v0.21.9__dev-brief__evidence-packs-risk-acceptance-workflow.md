## The Concept: The Vault as an Evidence Pack

The unit of work is not a file. It is a folder. A folder that contains the deliverable AND the evidence that supports it. The article AND the sources. The decision AND the reasoning chain. The risk acceptance AND the approval tree.

This is what I have been calling evidence packs, and vaults are the first technology that makes them practical. Every evidence pack is a vault: versioned, encrypted, shareable, browsable, with a page layout that creates a narrative through the materials.

The alternative (email chains, shared drives, scattered PDFs, meeting notes nobody reads) is what organisations use today. It is terrible. We can replace it with something that actually works.

Two use cases to build as proof-of-concepts and feature on the new website.

## Use Case 1: News Story Evidence Pack

### What It Is

A news article (or analysis, or report) delivered not as a standalone document but as a vault containing everything: the article itself, the sources, the evidence, the semantic knowledge graph, infographics, versions for multiple audiences, and the research trail.

### What the Vault Contains

```
news-story-vault/
    |
    +--> article/
    |       +--> article.md           (the main article)
    |       +--> article-executive.md (executive summary version)
    |       +--> article-technical.md (technical deep-dive version)
    |
    +--> infographics/
    |       +--> summary.svg          (visual summary)
    |       +--> key-findings.svg     (key findings infographic)
    |
    +--> evidence/
    |       +--> sources.json         (all source URLs with metadata)
    |       +--> source-1.pdf         (captured/archived source)
    |       +--> source-2.pdf
    |       +--> fact-check.md        (Perplexity evidence check)
    |
    +--> knowledge-graph/
    |       +--> entities.json        (semantic knowledge graph)
    |       +--> relationships.json
    |
    +--> _page.json                   (page layout: narrative navigation)
    +--> index.html                   (HTML presentation view)
```

### The Workflow to Create It

```
Source material (topic, brief, or raw data)
    |
    v
Research (Perplexity Sonar: articles with source links)
    |
    v
Article generation (Claude: structured article from research)
    |
    v
Multi-audience versions (Claude: executive, technical, general)
    |
    v
Evidence gathering (Perplexity: verify each claim, capture sources)
    |
    v
Infographic generation (PlaybookLM pipeline)
    |
    v
Knowledge graph extraction (structured entities and relationships)
    |
    v
Page layout creation (_page.json: narrative navigation)
    |
    v
All committed to vault, share token generated
```

### Why This Matters

This connects directly to the MyFeeds.AI vision: every news story backed by evidence, delivered as a navigable pack, not a standalone article. The vault is the delivery format. The page layout is the narrative. The evidence folder is the trust layer.

The Perplexity Sonar integration (from the task-focused components brief) is critical here: it is the only model that provides real source URLs. Every claim in the article links to a source. The sources are archived in the vault so they survive link rot.

### Connection to the Website

Feature this on sgraph.ai as a demo: "Here is a news analysis. Open the vault. Browse the article. Check the sources. View the infographic. All encrypted. All versioned." Provide a real vault token that visitors can try.

## Use Case 2: Risk Acceptance Workflow

### The Problem

Risk acceptance in organisations is broken. A decision gets made in a meeting. Someone writes it up (maybe). The reasoning is lost. The approval chain is informal. Six months later, nobody can explain why a particular risk was accepted, who approved it, what evidence was considered, or what alternatives were discussed.

This is not just a documentation problem. It is a governance failure. Regulators ask "show me the decision trail." Auditors ask "who signed off?" Incident responders ask "why was this risk accepted?" The answer is usually "we think it was discussed in a meeting, let me check my email."

### What a Risk Acceptance Vault Contains

```
risk-decision-vault/
    |
    +--> decision/
    |       +--> decision-summary.md     (the decision: what risk, accepted/rejected, conditions)
    |       +--> reasoning.md            (the chain of reasoning that led to the decision)
    |       +--> alternatives.md         (alternatives considered and why they were not chosen)
    |
    +--> evidence/
    |       +--> risk-assessment.md      (the original risk assessment)
    |       +--> threat-analysis.md      (threat landscape at time of decision)
    |       +--> impact-analysis.md      (business impact if risk materialises)
    |       +--> controls-in-place.md    (existing mitigations)
    |       +--> cost-analysis.md        (cost of mitigation vs cost of acceptance)
    |
    +--> approvals/
    |       +--> approval-tree.json      (the org chart of approvals: who reviewed, who accepted)
    |       +--> team-review.md          (team-level review: agreed/disagreed/abstained, with comments)
    |       +--> manager-review.md       (manager-level: accepted based on team input)
    |       +--> exec-review.md          (exec-level: final decision, overrides if any)
    |       +--> dissent.md              (any recorded disagreements, on the record)
    |
    +--> _page.json                      (page layout: guided navigation through the decision)
    +--> index.html                      (visualisation: approval tree, timeline, status)
```

### The Approval Tree

The key innovation here is the approval tree: a structured, navigable record of who reviewed the risk at each level, what their position was, and how the decision cascaded upward.

```
CEO: Accepted (overrode CISO concern)
  |
  +--> CTO: Accepted (conditional on Q3 review)
  |     |
  |     +--> Engineering Lead: Accepted (team consensus)
  |     |     |
  |     |     +--> Security Engineer A: Accepted
  |     |     +--> Security Engineer B: Disagreed (on record, concerns documented)
  |     |
  |     +--> DevOps Lead: Accepted (noted monitoring gap)
  |
  +--> CISO: Did not accept (concerns documented, overruled by CEO)
  |     |
  |     +--> Security Analyst: Did not accept (evidence of similar breaches)
  |
  +--> CFO: Accepted (cost analysis supports acceptance)
```

This tree is stored as JSON (`approval-tree.json`) and rendered as an interactive HTML visualisation (`index.html`). Each node links to the relevant review document. Dissent is explicitly captured and linked.

### Why the Vault Is Perfect for This

**Version control:** Every revision of the risk assessment, every update to the approval tree, every added piece of evidence is a commit. The complete history of the decision is preserved.

**Encryption:** Risk decisions often involve sensitive business information (threat intelligence, financial data, personnel decisions). Client-encrypted vaults mean this data is protected at rest.

**Multi-user collaboration:** The team lead commits their review. The manager commits their acceptance. The exec commits the final decision. Each person works on the same vault via the dual branch model. The commit history shows who contributed what and when.

**Auditability:** An auditor or regulator receives a share token. They browse the vault online (tree-only loading, fast). They can see the decision, the evidence, the approval tree, and the complete version history. Everything in one place, navigable, verifiable.

**Future: PGP signing.** When we add PKI/PGP to vaults, each approval becomes cryptographically signed. The approval tree becomes a chain of signatures. "The CISO signed their review at this timestamp with this key." Non-repudiation built into the workflow.

### The HTML Visualisation

The `index.html` in the vault renders an interactive view:

- The approval tree as a visual org chart with colour-coded status (green: accepted, red: not accepted, amber: conditional)
- Timeline of the decision process (when each review was submitted)
- Summary statistics (X of Y reviewers accepted, N conditions attached, M dissents recorded)
- Click any node to see the full review document

This HTML page loads data from files in the vault (using the vault's file-access-from-HTML capability shipped in the latest version). The visualisation is part of the evidence pack, not a separate tool.
