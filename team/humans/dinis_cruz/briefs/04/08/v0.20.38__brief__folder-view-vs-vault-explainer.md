# Website Explainer Needed: Folder View vs Vault

**version** v0.20.38
**date** 08 Apr 2026
**from** Human (project lead)
**to** Ambassador (lead), Designer, Developer
**type** Brief

---

## The Gap

During a partner call, the distinction between Folder View and Vault was not immediately clear to the user. The website needs a clear, visible explanation of the two concepts and when to use each.

## The Two Concepts

| Concept | What It Is | Access Model | Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Folder View** (Browser) | A read-only published snapshot of files. Static. Shareable via simple token. | Public, read-only | Sharing materials, browsing content, downloading files |
| **Vault** | A live, versioned, encrypted object store. Git-like operations (commit, push, pull, branch). | Authenticated, read-write | Collaboration, version control, agentic workflows, persistent workspace |

## Key Insight from the Call

When the partner said "I don't use the command line," the human was able to say "here's a vault online." The online vault view solved the access problem. But the user still needs to understand what they are looking at: a snapshot (folder) or a live workspace (vault).

## Action

Add an explainer section to the website (possibly on the landing page or as a help tooltip in the UI) that makes this distinction in plain language.
