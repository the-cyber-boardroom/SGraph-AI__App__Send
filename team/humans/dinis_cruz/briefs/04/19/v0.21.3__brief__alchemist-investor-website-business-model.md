## The Task

Create and launch investor.sgraph.ai: a dedicated site for investor materials, pitch decks, business model documentation, and supporting evidence. This is not a one-deck-fits-all approach. It is an iterating, multi-deck, feedback-driven materials library.

## Why Multiple Decks

We will not have one pitch deck. We will have many. Different audiences need different framings:

- Technical investors want the architecture story (zero-knowledge, serverless, run everywhere)
- Security-focused investors want the GDPR and data governance story
- Market-focused investors want the competitive positioning (vs Dropbox, WeTransfer, Git)
- AI-focused investors want the agentic workflow story (vaults as agent communication, PlaybookLM, five-tier intelligence model)

Each deck is a version, an experiment. The more we create, the better we get at finding what resonates. Early feedback is already coming in: people are saying "this is so cool that you share this information." That signal means we are on the right track. Now we need to scale it.

## What the Website Should Contain

### Pitch Decks (Multiple Versions)

Each deck should be a vault, browsable online via the page layout renderer. This is the meta-demo again: the investor consumes the pitch using the product being pitched.

Decks should cover at minimum:

- The product overview (what SG/Send does, for whom)
- The technology story (architecture, encryption, serverless)
- The market story (competitive positioning, use cases, traction)
- The business model (usage-based pricing, open source, deployment as the product)
- The why-now story (AI agents need encrypted communication, GDPR compliance, data sovereignty)

### Supporting Evidence

Each deck claim should be backed by evidence documents, research, case studies, and data. These live alongside the decks (in the same vault or linked vaults) so an interested investor can go deeper.

With the new capability of loading data from files into web pages (including from vaults), we can create rich, interactive evidence pages that are far more compelling than static PDFs.

### Infographics

Each key concept should have an infographic version: the marketing strategy, the go-to-market, the customer acquisition funnel, the feature set, the "why now." These are generated via the PlaybookLM infographic pipeline (sub-10-cent cost, 15 seconds per infographic). Create many, iterate fast.

The problem with past attempts (Cyber Boardroom era) was that the documents existed but were hard to consume. Infographics solve this. One visual that communicates the core message, backed by a vault of supporting documents for those who want depth.

### Videos

We can now create videos relatively easily (client-side recording, screen capture, presentation mode). Short walkthrough videos of key workflows are powerful investor materials. "Here is the product in 90 seconds" is more compelling than 20 slides.

## Leverage Existing Work

A lot of the business model thinking has not changed in 18 months. The Cyber Boardroom business plan (available in the GitHub repo) has been used to create early versions of the SG/Send business plan. Do not reinvent this. Leverage the existing materials:

- Cyber Boardroom business plan and strategy documents
- Previous Alchemist-created business plan versions
- The OWASP presentation pack (8 articles covering core primitives, shipping, open source model, investment strategy)
- The 12 LinkedIn articles (traction evidence)
- The 251+ briefs (decision trail evidence)

Map the history. The journey is the story. Show how the thinking evolved from Cyber Boardroom to SG/Send, what changed and what stayed the same. Investors who see a coherent 18-month arc of thinking are more confident than those who see a polished deck with no history.

## Publication Model

Use the website + vault combination:

- **Website** (investor.sgraph.ai): the public face. Curated, designed, professional.
- **Vaults**: the depth. Each deck is a vault. Supporting evidence in linked vaults. Investors who want to go deeper clone the vault or browse online.
- **Share tokens**: read-only snapshots for specific investor conversations. "Here is the deck I prepared for our meeting: [token]."

This demonstrates the product while delivering the pitch. The medium is the message.

## Iteration Model

Do not try to get it perfect. Ship the first version with whatever is ready. Add decks as we create them. Update materials as feedback comes in. The investor site should follow the same "ship it" principle as everything else: live is better than polished, and feedback-driven is better than assumption-driven.
