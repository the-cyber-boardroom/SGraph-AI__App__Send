# AWS Outreach Workflow: Personalised Briefings at Scale

**date** 20 Apr 2026
**from** Human (project lead)
**to** Sherpa (lead), Ambassador, Developer
**type** Brief

## The Need

Before, during, and after the AWS Conference on Wednesday, I need to reach out to specific people (many of whom I have personal relationships with) with materials that are personalised to them, their company, and how SG/Send can work with what they do.

This cannot be generic. A one-size-fits-all deck will not cut it. Each outreach needs to show: "I understand your company. Here is how this technology maps to your world. Try it."

The question is: how do we make this fast enough that I can do it for 10-20 contacts without spending a full day on each one?

## The Workflow

### Input

I type a name, a company, or an email address. That is it. The system does the rest.

### What Happens Behind the Scenes

1. **Research:** Pull information about the person and their company. Use Perplexity for web research, LinkedIn for context, any existing notes or previous conversations.

2. **Mapping:** Identify how SG/Send's capabilities map to their company's needs. What industry are they in? What security/data governance challenges do they likely have? Which of our use cases (encrypted file sharing, vault collaboration, agent communication, Playwright automation, healthcare workflows) is most relevant?

3. **Briefing generation:** Create a personalised briefing: a short document (or vault) that covers who they are, what we do, why it matters to them specifically, and a concrete next step (try this vault, look at this demo, schedule a call).

4. **Delivery:** Package the briefing as a vault with a share token. Optionally send via email with the token and a one-line hook.

### Output

A personalised vault for each contact containing:

- A one-page briefing tailored to their company and role
- The relevant use case(s) with screenshots or demo links
- The architecture overview (AWS-focused if they are AWS people)
- A "try it now" section with a live token they can use immediately
- Optionally: an infographic summarising the value proposition for their specific context

## How to Implement This

### Option A: Pre-Primed Claude Sessions

Have one or two Claude web sessions already set up with:

- Vault credentials (so the session can create and populate vaults)
- The SG/Send positioning brief and use case library
- Access to research tools (Perplexity, web search)
- Templates for the personalised briefing format

I type: "Create a briefing for [name] at [company]." The session researches, generates, packages into a vault, and gives me the share token. I send the token.

### Option B: Tool on tools.sgraph.ai

A dedicated tool where I type a company name or individual, and it:

- Runs a research pipeline (Perplexity or web search for company info)
- Generates the personalised briefing using an LLM
- Creates a vault with the materials
- Returns a share token

This is more automated but requires building the tool. For Wednesday, Option A (pre-primed sessions) is more realistic. Option B is the productised version for ongoing outreach.

### Option C: Email Integration

Extend either option to send the email directly: compose a short personalised email with the vault token, send it. The recipient clicks the link, opens the vault, sees a personalised briefing about how SG/Send maps to their world.

This is the "one analysis, many audiences" principle from the Cyber Boardroom: same underlying platform knowledge, personalised framing per recipient.

## For Wednesday (Minimum Viable Outreach)

Given the time constraint, here is the minimum:

1. **Prepare a list of 10-20 target contacts** for the AWS event (people I know, people I want to meet)
2. **Set up one pre-primed Claude web session** with vault credentials and the briefing template
3. **Before the event:** Generate personalised briefings for the top 5 contacts I most want to reach. Send via email with tokens.
4. **During the event:** When I meet someone interesting, note their name/company. Generate a briefing on the spot (phone, quick session) or immediately after the conversation.
5. **After the event:** Follow up with everyone I met using personalised vaults within 24 hours.

## The Meta-Demo

Every outreach is itself a demo of the product. The recipient receives a share token. They open a vault. They browse personalised materials in a page layout. They see the encryption indicator. They experience version-controlled, client-encrypted vaults for humans.

The medium is the message. The outreach method demonstrates the product being pitched.
