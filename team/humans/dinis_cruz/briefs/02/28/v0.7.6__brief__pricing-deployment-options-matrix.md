## Two Modes: Free and Commercial

The pricing page should present two clear sections. The free offerings demonstrate our commitment to open source and promote adoption. The commercial offerings are where revenue comes from.

---

## Section 1: Free Offerings

These are genuinely free — no catch, no "free tier that expires." The goal is to promote usage and lower barriers to adoption, especially for partners, enterprises evaluating the technology, and developers.

### Option A: Early Access Programme (EAP)

**Who it's for:** Anyone who wants to try SG/Send without paying. Individuals, teams evaluating the product, curious technologists.

**What they get:**
- An access token with limited credits (enough to evaluate the product)
- Full access to the send.sgraph.ai hosted service
- Same zero-knowledge encryption as paid tiers
- No payment required, no credit card needed

**How it works:** Sign up on the website, Sherpa responds within 48 hours with an access token. Credits are limited — once used up, upgrade to the Starter pack (£5) or deploy open source.

**Call to action:** "Join the Early Access Programme — Free" → sign-up form / email.

### Option B: Open Source — Self-Hosted

**Who it's for:** Developers, companies who want full control, partners who want to deploy for their customers.

**What they get:**
- Clone the repo from GitHub
- Deploy in their own environment (their servers, their cloud, their laptop)
- Their keys, their data, their infrastructure
- No dependency on SG/Send infrastructure
- Free forever — it's open source

**Call to action:** "Free — it's open source" → links to a page explaining: the repo is public, the licence allows commercial use, here's the quickstart guide, here's how to deploy.

### Option C: Self-Hosted on Own Cloud Account

**Who it's for:** Companies who want to run SG/Send in their own AWS/GCP/Azure account with their own billing relationship with the cloud provider.

**What they get:**
- Deploy using our PyPI packages or source code
- Runs in their AWS account, their S3 buckets, their Lambda functions
- They pay their cloud provider directly for infrastructure costs
- Full isolation — nothing touches SG/Send infrastructure
- Free from us — they only pay their cloud provider

**Call to action:** "Free — deploy in your cloud" → links to deployment guide.

### Option D: White Label / Partner Deployment

**Who it's for:** Partners who want to offer SG/Send functionality to their own customers under their own brand.

**What they get:**
- Same as Option B but deployed for their end customers
- Can customise branding, logos, domain
- Can integrate into their existing product
- No licensing fee from SG/Send

**Call to action:** "Free for partners" → links to a page explaining the partner model, with "Contact Sherpa for deployment support."

### Why Free?

Each free option should link to a page that explains:

- SG/Send is open source — the code is public and the licence permits commercial use
- Our mission is to make encrypted file transfer ubiquitous
- The more deployments that exist, the more secure the ecosystem becomes
- We make money from the hosted service, not from licensing the software
- If you want us to run it for you, that's where the commercial options come in

## Section 2: Commercial Offerings — SG/Send Cloud

The commercial offerings are the hosted service — "we run it, you use it." Branded as **SG/Send Cloud**.

### Tier 1: Multi-Tenant (SG/Send Cloud — Shared)

**What it is:** The current send.sgraph.ai service. Shared infrastructure, shared Lambda functions, shared S3 buckets. Every user's encrypted files are in the same infrastructure (but each file is encrypted with its own key — so shared infrastructure does not mean shared access).

**Available:** Today.

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Infrastructure** | Shared (multi-tenant) |
| **Encryption** | Zero-knowledge, client-side — same as all modes |
| **Pricing** | £5 for 500 file transfers (£0.01/file) |
| **Credit expiry** | 3 months |
| **Payment** | Stripe (card, Apple Pay, Google Pay) |
| **Support** | Email (Sherpa) |
| **SLA** | Best effort |

**Call to action:** "Buy Credits — £5" → Stripe checkout.

### Tier 2: Single-Tenant (SG/Send Cloud — Dedicated)

**What it is:** A dedicated deployment running in SG/Send's AWS account, but with isolated infrastructure for the customer. Their own S3 buckets, their own Lambda functions, their own CloudFront distribution. An exact replica of the multi-tenant solution, but all dedicated to them.

**Available:** Contact us for pricing.

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Infrastructure** | Dedicated (single-tenant) — isolated S3, Lambda, CloudFront |
| **Encryption** | Zero-knowledge, client-side — same as all modes |
| **Hosting** | SG/Send's AWS account, managed by us |
| **Customisation** | Custom domain, logo, branding possible |
| **Pricing** | Contact us (not yet determined) |
| **Support** | Dedicated email support |
| **SLA** | Custom |

**Why this matters for security:** Single-tenant means the customer's encrypted data is in infrastructure that no other customer shares. Even though the multi-tenant mode is already zero-knowledge (so shared infrastructure doesn't expose data), some customers — especially in regulated industries — require dedicated infrastructure for compliance reasons.

**Future billing model (not yet implemented):** Single-tenant introduces more granular billable units beyond per-file pricing:

| Billable Unit | What It Covers |
|---|---|
| Per file transfer | Same as multi-tenant (usage-based) |
| DNS entry | Custom domain (e.g., send.customer.com) |
| Storage | S3 storage for encrypted files |
| Bandwidth | CloudFront egress |
| Platform fee | Monthly base fee for dedicated infrastructure |

These are transparent — the customer sees exactly what they're paying for and why.

**Call to action:** "Contact us for pricing" → Sherpa email (sherpa@sgraph.ai), eventually Ambassador email, eventually a contact form.

### Tier 3: AWS Marketplace

**What it is:** Deploy SG/Send via the AWS Marketplace. Pay-per-usage through AWS billing. The customer deploys in their own AWS account but purchases through the marketplace listing.

**Available:** Coming soon.

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Infrastructure** | Customer's AWS account |
| **Billing** | Through AWS Marketplace (consolidated billing) |
| **Pricing** | Pay per usage (TBD) |
| **Deployment** | One-click from AWS Marketplace |
| **Support** | Via AWS Marketplace support channel |

**Why this matters:** Many enterprises prefer purchasing through AWS Marketplace because it counts against their AWS committed spend, simplifies procurement (no new vendor onboarding), and consolidates billing.

**Call to action:** "Coming soon — register interest" → email.

### Tier 4: Bring Your Own Cloud (Managed Deployment)

**What it is:** SG/Send is deployed in the customer's cloud account, managed or assisted by us. Nothing runs in SG/Send's infrastructure. We help with deployment, configuration, and ongoing management.

**Available:** Contact us.

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Infrastructure** | 100% customer's cloud account |
| **Management** | SG/Send assists with deployment and configuration |
| **Data residency** | Customer has full control — their region, their compliance |
| **Pricing** | Contact us (professional services + optional managed service) |
| **Support** | Dedicated |

**Call to action:** "Contact us" → Sherpa email.

## Security Consistency Across All Modes

Every mode — free or paid, multi-tenant or self-hosted — uses the same encryption. This should be prominently stated on the pricing page:

```
🔐 Every mode uses the same zero-knowledge encryption.

   Whether you use our hosted service or deploy in your own 
   environment, files are encrypted in the browser before they 
   leave your device. We never see your data. Your cloud provider
   never sees your data. Nobody sees your data except you and 
   your recipient.

   The only difference between modes is who manages the 
   infrastructure. The encryption is identical.
```
