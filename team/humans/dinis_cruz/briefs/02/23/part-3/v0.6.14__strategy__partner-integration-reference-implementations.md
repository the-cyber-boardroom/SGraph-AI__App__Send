## The Funnel

```
Visitors → Users → Customers → Investors
```

This is the line. Partners accelerate every transition: they bring visitors (through their market), convert them to users (through integration), monetise them as customers (through billing), and validate the technology for investors (through adoption).

---

## What Partners Need

A partner considering integration needs to see:

1. **The use case** — how their product benefits from SG/Send integration (specific to their market)
2. **The architecture** — how the integration works technically (reference implementation)
3. **The effort** — how much work it is to integrate (ideally: minimal)
4. **The economics** — how they make money from it (billing model, value proposition)

We need to create this documentation FOR EACH PARTNER TYPE and publish it on our website. Not generic "partner with us" pages — specific, technical, architectural reference implementations.

## Partner Types and Use Cases

| Partner Type | Their Product | Our Value Add | Their Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Secure browsers** (Surf Security, etc.) | Browser security | "Only open this in [Secure Browser]" — we promote their product as a requirement | Per-user or per-session fee when our users use their browser |
| **File scanning / CDR** (Votiro, OPSWAT, etc.) | File security | We integrate their scanning into our chain. Every file our users send can pass through their service. | Per-file scanning fee, billed through our platform |
| **Identity providers** (Okta, Entra ID, etc.) | Identity verification | Their verified identities map to our PKI keys. Enterprise SSO for data rooms. | Enterprise licence fees, identity verification fees |
| **Cloud storage** (AWS, Azure, Dropbox, etc.) | Storage | Our encryption layer sits on top of their storage. Their customers get zero-knowledge encryption. | Storage fees (unchanged), premium for encrypted storage |
| **Email providers** (Gmail, ProtonMail, etc.) | Communication | PKI integration adds verifiable identity to their email. Our trust web extends to their users. | Premium tier for encrypted email, enterprise features |
| **Legal tech** (contract management, e-signing) | Document workflow | Fractal document signing, per-clause provenance, EULA verification | Per-document or per-signature fees |
| **Supply chain platforms** | Data exchange | Portable data rooms travel through the supply chain. Each participant adds signed data. | Per-transaction or per-data-room fees |
| **Privacy organisations** | Trust / review | Sign and verify EULA clauses, content verification, trust certification | Micropayments per review, subscription for verification services |

## Documentation Structure Per Partner

For each partner type, publish on our website:

```
sgraph.ai/partners/{partner-type}/
├── overview.md          ← "Why integrate SG/Send with [partner type]"
│                          2-3 paragraphs. The business case.
├── architecture.md      ← Reference architecture diagram
│                          How the integration works technically.
│                          API endpoints, data flow, key management.
├── implementation.md    ← Step-by-step integration guide
│                          Code samples, SDK usage, test scenarios.
├── economics.md         ← Revenue model
│                          How the partner makes money. Billing integration.
└── demo/                ← Working demo
                           A live example of the integration.
                           (Connects to the read-only demo environments)
```

### What the Alchemist Builds

For each priority partner type, create the architectural reference implementation — not just documentation but working code that demonstrates the integration. This is what the human can point partners to: "here's how it works, and here's a running demo."

## Core Primitives to Productise

The business sits on three primitives. Partner integrations map to one or more:

| Primitive | What It Is | Which Partners Use It |
|---|---|---|
| **Encrypted transport** | PKI-based send/receive without server seeing content | Email providers, messaging, cloud storage, SaaS platforms |
| **Data vault** | Encrypted storage with access control, versioning, audit | Cloud storage, legal tech, supply chain, enterprise collaboration |
| **Trust workflows** | Scanning chains, trust web, dual authorization, provenance | Scanning services, identity providers, privacy organisations, secure browsers |

Every partner integration uses one or more of these primitives. The primitives are the product. The partner integrations are the distribution channels.

---

## Multi-Language, Multi-Culture Priority

A key competitive advantage: the platform must work natively across languages and cultures. Most encryption and PKI tools are English-only. Partners in non-English markets (DACH, France, Japan, Latin America, Middle East) are particularly valuable because they face LESS competition.

The Ambassador should prioritise partners who operate in multiple languages or in markets underserved by English-first solutions.
