## The Strategy

SG/Send should stay focused: encrypted transfer, vaults, CLI, the PKI-signed encrypted Git. That is the core. The centre of gravity should not drift toward building every possible use case.

But the use cases are real. Document analysis, investor relationships, mortgages, medical information, supply chain management, data contracts, accountancy workflows, agent-to-agent communication. Each of these is a company waiting to be built.

The play: encourage other entrepreneurs to build those companies on top of SG/Send primitives. Not as employees, not as contractors. As founders of their own companies, with their own ownership, their own markets, their own customers.

## Why This Works for Everyone

**For the entrepreneur:** They come with domain expertise, industry knowledge, and a problem to solve. They do not have to build encryption, file transfer, vaults, version control, or billing. SG/Send provides those building blocks. They focus on what they know: the market, the workflow, the customer relationship.

**For SG/Send:** Every company built on the primitives increases usage, battle-hardens the technology, and makes the core brand more valuable. The more companies use the encryption pipeline, the vaults, the CLI, the more those components get tested in real-world conditions. The ecosystem creates value that a single company never could.

**For the conversation:** When meeting potential co-founders who have their own ideas and their own centre of gravity, the conversation changes. Instead of "join SG/Send and work on my thing," it becomes "build your thing on top of what we have built." This is a much easier conversation. It gives them maximum ownership, maximum autonomy, and a head start on infrastructure they would otherwise spend months building.

## The Three Primitives They Get

### 1. Encrypted File Transfer (Client-Side)

Not just browser encryption. Client encryption across every environment: browser, desktop, mobile, CLI, and AI agents (Claude installs `sg-send-cli` from PyPI and goes). Every one of these is a client. Symmetric and PKI encryption (public/private keys), which matters for the more sophisticated use cases.

### 2. Folder and Zip Sharing

A zip that can be sent, viewed in the folder UI, downloaded, and forwarded. For many workflows, this is enough. Sequential workflows (someone receives data, adds to it, sends to the next person) do not need collaboration. They need distribution. Accountants, compliance teams, document review chains: zips flying around on emails or through SG/Send, decrypted on arrival. Simple, effective.

The folder view UI is getting really good. This is a standalone value proposition: share a structured folder that someone can browse and selectively download from.

### 3. Vaults (Collaboration)

When zips are not enough, vaults provide multi-user collaboration with the PKI-signed encrypted Git: versioning, branches, merges, signed commits. This is for workflows where multiple people edit the same data over time. Data rooms, project collaboration, ongoing document management.

The distinction matters: zip is distribution, vault is collaboration. Different use cases, different primitives. Some businesses will only need zips. Some will need vaults. The architecture supports both.

## The GDPR and Provenance Angle

For many of these businesses, the data handling problem is the hard problem. Where is the data stored? Who has access? How do you prove provenance? How do you comply with GDPR?

SG/Send solves this in a single move: whoever has the decryption key can read the data. Nobody else can. Even if the encrypted data is stored in a different geographical location, it does not matter, because the storage location does not have the decryption key. Provenance is cryptographic: who signed it, who has the key. Not policy-based, not access-control-list-based. Cryptographic.

For a startup building in healthcare, finance, legal, or any regulated industry, this removes months of compliance work.

## The Founder Pack

Create a "Build Your Company on SG/Send" pack on the website. This is a vault (naturally) containing:

### Business Template
- Recommended business model (serverless, low cost to run, recurring revenue, built-in billing)
- Open source sovereignty play (no lock-in, customers can deploy their own)
- Pricing template (based on the SG/Send credit model)
- Market entry framework: "can you enter this market and make something that exists today better?"

### Technical Template
- Dev pack: everything needed to start a new project in Claude Code from one repo
- Role definitions for the agentic team (Explorer, Villager, Town Planner teams)
- CI/CD pipeline setup
- Component library access (tools.sgraph.ai)
- Vault integration guide
- CLI integration guide

### Agentic Team Template
- The 18+ agent role definitions, adapted for a new project
- Conductor, Architect, Developer, Designer, Sherpa, QA, AppSec roles
- Explorer/Villager/Town Planner team structure
- Brief and debrief workflow template

This pack could itself be a revenue item. SG/Send could charge for customised dev packs, or offer them free as an ecosystem investment. Either way, lowering the barrier for new companies to start building on the primitives increases the ecosystem value.

## Feature Request: Encrypted File Extension

Captured from the memo: explore a standalone encrypted file format that can be emailed as an attachment and opened by the SG/Send client (desktop, mobile, browser).

Options to explore:
- `.vault` extension (a single encrypted blob or zip)
- `.sgenc` or similar custom extension
- A zip containing an encrypted binary with metadata
- File association on desktop/mobile: double-click opens in SG/Send client

This enables the "email a vault" workflow: create an encrypted package, attach it to an email, recipient opens it in their SG/Send client with the decryption key shared via a separate channel.

Separate brief recommended for the technical exploration.
