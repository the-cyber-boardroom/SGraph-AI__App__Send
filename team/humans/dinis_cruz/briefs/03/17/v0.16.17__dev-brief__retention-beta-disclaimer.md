## Retention Period Selector

Add a retention period selector to the upload flow. The user chooses how long their files are kept on the server:

| Option | Default | Use Case |
|---|---|---|
| 1 day | | Quick transfers, sensitive data, one-time shares |
| 3 days | ✓ (default) | Standard transfers |
| 7 days | | Longer availability for recipients in different time zones |
| 30 days | | Ongoing access, shared references |

After the selected period, encrypted data is deleted from the server. The user is told this upfront. No ambiguity.

### Why This Matters

- **For the user:** Clear expectation of how long the data is available. They choose based on their needs.
- **For us:** S3 storage can be pruned automatically. Files with 1-day retention are cleaned up daily. This keeps storage costs manageable as usage grows.
- **For the product signal:** Tracking which retention periods users select tells us how people use the service. If 80% choose 30 days, that tells us something different than if 80% choose 1 day.
- **For vaults:** Vaults are different. Vault data is persistent by default (that is the point of a vault). The retention selector applies to standard file transfers and galleries, not to vault contents.

### Implementation

Add a simple dropdown or button group to the upload flow (fits into Step 2 of the three-step UX redesign). Store the retention period as metadata alongside the encrypted file. A scheduled Lambda or cron job deletes expired files from S3.

## Beta Disclaimer

Add a visible disclaimer to the service. Placement: on the upload page and in the footer.

**Content (draft):**

> SG/Send is currently in beta. The service is under active development. While we work to ensure reliability, service disruptions may occur. Files uploaded to SG/Send are for temporary transfer, not long-term storage. Do not upload files that you cannot afford to lose. Files will be deleted after your selected retention period. We may also delete files earlier in the event of service maintenance.

### Key Points

- This is a beta service, under active development
- Service disruptions may occur
- This is for temporary transfer, not storage
- Do not upload irreplaceable data
- Files are deleted after the retention period (and possibly sooner)
- Vaults have separate retention semantics (persistent until the user deletes them or their account expires)

### Tone

Honest and direct. Not buried in legalese. Not scary. The message is: "we are building this fast, we are being transparent about where we are, and we want you to use it knowing what to expect." This aligns with the six-sentence privacy policy ethos: say it plainly.
