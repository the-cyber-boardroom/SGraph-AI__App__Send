## The Workflow

A concrete, personal workflow that demonstrates the technology in a way anyone can understand. The pattern:

1. Start with a local folder (Git-managed or not)
2. Create a vault on it
3. Share the vault key with Claude
4. Claude makes changes: improves descriptions, consolidates information, adds structure
5. Sync locally, review, iterate
6. When happy: close the vault, create a zip/folder
7. Use SG/Send to share the folder (get a friendly link)
8. Share the friendly link with the recipient (accountant, doctor, agent)

The key insight: the LLM is used during preparation (improving the materials before sharing) but is not inline during the sharing itself. By the time the recipient gets the link, the materials have already been enriched, structured, and reviewed.

Two levels of version control throughout: the vault tracks changes during collaboration with Claude, and Git tracks commits of the reviewed output.

## Use Case 1: Tax Returns for My Accountant

**The scenario:** Preparing and sharing tax documents with an accountant.

**What goes in the folder:**
- Financial statements and bank records
- Receipts and invoices
- Previous year's tax return
- Company information and registration documents
- A summary document (created/improved by the LLM)
- Notes on specific items that need attention

**How the LLM helps during preparation:**
- Consolidates scattered financial information into a clear summary
- Cross-references receipts against bank statements
- Identifies missing documents ("you have an invoice but no corresponding bank statement for X")
- Creates a cover letter explaining what is included and what the accountant needs to review
- Formats everything consistently

**What the accountant receives:**
A single link to a folder/gallery containing all materials, well-organised, with a clear summary and cover letter. The accountant opens the link, browses the folder view, sees everything at a glance, and can download what they need.

**The value over email:** No size limits, no scattered attachments across 10 emails, organised structure, and the accountant can see if specific documents have been viewed or downloaded.

## Use Case 2: Medical Records for My Doctor

**The scenario:** Sharing knee-related medical records and history with a doctor.

**What goes in the folder:**
- Previous scan results (MRI, X-ray images)
- Doctor's notes and reports from prior consultations
- A personal description of the condition and symptoms
- Medication history
- Photos or videos showing range of motion
- A timeline of the condition

**How the LLM helps during preparation:**
- Creates a structured patient summary from scattered notes
- Organises the timeline chronologically
- Describes the condition in clear medical terminology (from the patient's informal description)
- Identifies gaps ("you mention a scan in January but no results are included")
- Creates a one-page overview for the doctor to read first

**What the doctor receives:**
A single link to a folder containing the complete medical history for the knee, well-structured, with a summary page on top. The doctor opens it, reads the summary, and drills into specific documents as needed.

**The value over the current system:** Medical records are notoriously hard to share. Patients carry USB sticks, email password-protected zips, or physically hand over CDs. A single encrypted link with a gallery view of all materials is dramatically better.

**The privacy angle:** Zero-knowledge encryption means even SG/Send cannot read the medical records. The doctor receives the link and the decryption key. Nobody else can access the content. This is a genuine privacy advantage over email, cloud storage, or USB sticks.
