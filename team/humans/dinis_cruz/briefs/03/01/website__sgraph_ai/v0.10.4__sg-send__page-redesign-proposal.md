---

## Design Direction

The four roles converged on a shared set of structural principles during the UX brief. This proposal translates those principles into concrete page layouts. The goal is not a visual redesign — the existing dark theme, typography, and colour palette are strong. The goal is an information architecture restructuring so that the page's form follows the user's function, not the builder's org chart.

**The key structural move:** On every page, primary actions come first, explanations come second. Users who arrive with intent can act immediately. Users who need convincing can scroll. Both are served. Neither blocks the other.

**Constraint acknowledged:** The eight pricing options stay on the pricing page — they communicate the open-source philosophy and partner-friendliness of the project. But they move below the two primary CTAs, not above them.

### What Changed and Why

| Change | Principle | Role rationale |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| "Buy Credits — £5" is now primary CTA (filled, left) | Primary action = most reachable | **Ambassador:** Challenge campaign's #1 conversion goal. **Designer:** Hierarchy must match priority. |
| "Buy Credits" links directly to Stripe | Every click must earn its existence | **Sherpa:** Eliminates 2 intermediate pages and 3 scrolls. **Advocate:** Intentful Buyer persona can act immediately. |
| "Try Free" scrolls to inline signup form | Don't make me scroll past why to get to how | **Designer:** The form IS the destination — no reason for it to be on a separate page. **Sherpa:** Eliminates the entire `/pricing/free/` → `/early-access/` → LaunchList chain. |
| Signup form is inline on the homepage | Never break the trust chain | **Advocate:** User stays in the SG/Send experience. No third-party redirect. **Designer:** Form follows function — the form is two fields, it fits inline. |
| Explanation sections move below actions | The site should teach by doing, not explaining | **Designer:** Intent-driven users skip to action. Curious users scroll to learn. Neither blocks the other. |
| "Deploy Anywhere" teaser replaces full pricing grid on homepage | Form follows function, not org chart | **Ambassador:** Signals open-source and partner-friendly without 8-card decision paralysis. Links to full pricing page for those who want depth. |
| "Pricing" moves earlier in nav (after Product) | Primary action = most reachable | **Sherpa:** Users looking for pricing shouldn't have to scan past Team and Architecture first. |

## Page 4: `/pricing/cloud/` — Proposed: ELIMINATE

This page currently exists as a detail page for the Multi-Tenant £5 option. It contains "What's Included" cards and "How Credits Work" steps, all of which sit above the actual buy button.

**Proposal:** Delete this page entirely. Its content is absorbed into:
- "How Credits Work" → compact row on the pricing page (shown above)
- "What's Included" features → the Multi-Tenant card on the pricing page already lists these
- The buy button → every "Buy Credits" link goes directly to Stripe

If we want to preserve a "learn more about the cloud service" page for SEO or deep-linking, it can exist — but the buy button on it must be above the fold, not below two sections of cards.

## Page 5: `/pricing/free/` — Proposed: ELIMINATE

This page currently shows the philosophy section and a "Ready to get started?" section with buttons that go to further sub-pages. Its only unique content is the philosophy cards, which are now on the main pricing page.

**Proposal:** Delete this page. Redirect `/pricing/free/` to `/pricing/#free` (an anchor on the pricing page scrolling to the free tier section). The Early Access signup form now lives inline wherever the CTA appears.

---

## Page 6: `/early-access/` — Proposed: ELIMINATE

This page currently hosts the signup form. Once the form is inline on the homepage and pricing page, this page becomes redundant.

**Proposal:** Delete this page. No redirect needed — there are no external links to it. The signup form lives inline wherever it's needed — not on its own page.

## Navigation Changes

### Current

```
SG/  Product  Team  Architecture  Pricing  [EN-GB]
```

### Proposed

```
SG/  Product  Pricing  Architecture  Team  [EN-GB]
```

**Rationale:** "Pricing" is the second most likely destination after the homepage for a Challenge participant. Moving it earlier in the nav reduces scanning time. "Architecture" and "Team" are deep-dive content — they belong later in the nav order. This follows the Designer's hierarchy principle: the most important thing should be the most visible.
