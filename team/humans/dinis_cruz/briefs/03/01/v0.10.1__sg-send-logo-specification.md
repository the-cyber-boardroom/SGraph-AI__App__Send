# SG/Send — Logo Specification

**Version:** v0.10.1  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Status:** Active — adopted as primary brand identity  
**Concept:** C — S/Slash Monogram + Wordmark Lockup

---

## 1. Overview

The SG/Send logo is a two-part identity system: an **icon mark** (Concept C) for use as a favicon and app icon, paired with a **typographic wordmark** for the full brand lockup. Together they communicate the product's core promise: *secure, encrypted file transfer*.

The design distils the brand to its two most recognisable elements: the **S-curve** (representing the "S" in SG and the flowing motion of data in transit) and the **slash** (the defining punctuation of the SG/ brand system, representing the zero-knowledge encryption boundary).

---

## 2. Icon Mark — Concept C

### 2.1 Anatomy

The icon mark is composed of four layered elements within a 32×32 viewBox on a rounded dark navy rectangle:

| Element | Colour | Purpose |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Background** | Dark Navy `#1A1A2E`, `rx="7"` | Grounding container with rounded corners matching the app icon conventions |
| **S-curve** | Teal `#4ECDC4`, stroke-width 3, 90% opacity | The primary letterform — a flowing cubic Bézier S-path representing data in motion and the "S" of SG/Send |
| **Slash** | Coral `#E94560`, stroke-width 2.5 | The brand mark — a bold diagonal line bisecting the S-curve, representing the encryption boundary that separates user data from the server |
| **Encryption dots** | Slate `#8892A0`, 20–30% opacity | Four subtle dots in opposing corners suggesting data points, binary state, or cryptographic noise — a visual whisper of the zero-knowledge architecture |

### 2.2 Symbolism

The icon tells a layered story depending on the viewer's familiarity with the product:

**At a glance:** A bold, recognisable mark with a distinctive S-shape and cutting diagonal. It reads as dynamic and technical.

**With context:** The S-curve is data flowing between two parties. The coral slash cuts through it — this is the encryption boundary. What passes through the slash is transformed; the server never sees the original. The dots in the corners are the encrypted residue: present but unintelligible.

**The interplay:** The S-curve sits *behind* the slash in some places and *in front* in others, creating a visual weave. This suggests that encryption isn't a wall — it's integrated into the flow of data itself.

### 2.3 Design Decisions

**Why coral for the slash, not teal?** In the icon mark, the slash uses coral (`#E94560`) to create visual contrast against the teal S-curve and to draw the eye to the brand's most distinctive element. Per the design guidelines, coral represents "what we never see" — the zero-knowledge boundary. The slash *is* that boundary.

**Why reduced opacity on the S-curve?** At 90% opacity, the S-curve subtly recedes behind the slash, establishing a clear visual hierarchy: slash first, letterform second.

**Why dots instead of other decorative elements?** The dots are the most minimal possible suggestion of "data." They avoid being literal (no lock icons, no shield shapes) while adding visual balance to the corners and preventing the icon from feeling top-heavy.

## 3. Wordmark Lockup

### 3.1 Composition

The full lockup places the Concept C icon to the left of the typographic wordmark:

```
[Icon]  SG/Send
         ↑  ↑  ↑
         │  │  └── "Send": DM Sans Medium 500, Light Grey #E0E0E0
         │  └──── Slash: Custom 75° stroke, Teal #4ECDC4, 1.2× weight
         └────── "SG": DM Sans Bold 700, Light Grey #E0E0E0
```

### 3.2 Typography

| Part | Font | Weight | Colour | Notes |
|------|------|--------|--------|-------|
| **SG** | DM Sans | 700 (Bold) | `#E0E0E0` Light Grey | Brand prefix, heavier weight for emphasis |
| **/** | Custom stroke | 1.2× letterform weight | `#4ECDC4` Teal | Not a font glyph — drawn as a 75° angled line per brand guidelines |
| **Send** | DM Sans | 500 (Medium) | `#E0E0E0` Light Grey | Product name, lighter weight to establish hierarchy |

### 3.3 The Slash in the Wordmark

Per the SG/Send Design Guidelines (v0.3.5), the slash in the wordmark follows specific rules:

- **Angle:** 75° from horizontal (steeper than a standard `/` at ~68°)
- **Weight:** 1.2× the stroke weight of the surrounding DM Sans letterforms
- **Colour:** Teal (`#4ECDC4`) on dark backgrounds
- **Spacing:** Optically centred between "SG" and "Send", tighter than a normal space
- **Height:** Extends from baseline to cap height of the "S"
- **Caps:** Rounded stroke-linecap for consistency with DM Sans's geometric softness

### 3.4 Why Teal for the Wordmark Slash?

In the wordmark, the slash uses teal rather than coral. Without the teal S-curve present in the text, there is no need for contrast differentiation. The teal slash on the dark background provides strong legibility and connects the wordmark to the brand's primary accent colour as specified in the guidelines: *"Teal is the hero. It marks interactive elements and the slash."*

## 6. SVG Source Code

### 6.1 Icon Mark (32×32 Favicon)

```svg
<svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" viewBox="0 0 32 32">
  <rect width="32" height="32" rx="7" fill="#1A1A2E"/>

  <!-- S-curve: teal, flowing Bézier path -->
  <path d="M20 9 C20 9 22 9 22 11.5
           C22 14 18 14.5 16 14.5
           C14 14.5 10 15 10 17.5
           C10 20 12 21 14 21"
        fill="none"
        stroke="#4ECDC4"
        stroke-width="3"
        stroke-linecap="round"
        opacity="0.9"/>

  <!-- Slash: coral, the encryption boundary -->
  <line x1="20" y1="5" x2="12" y2="27"
        stroke="#E94560"
        stroke-width="2.5"
        stroke-linecap="round"/>

  <!-- Encryption dots: slate, subtle data noise -->
  <circle cx="7"  cy="7"  r="1" fill="#8892A0" opacity="0.3"/>
  <circle cx="7"  cy="11" r="1" fill="#8892A0" opacity="0.2"/>
  <circle cx="25" cy="21" r="1" fill="#8892A0" opacity="0.3"/>
  <circle cx="25" cy="25" r="1" fill="#8892A0" opacity="0.2"/>
</svg>
```

### 6.2 Element Breakdown

**Background rectangle:**
```svg
<rect width="32" height="32" rx="7" fill="#1A1A2E"/>
```
A 32×32 rounded rectangle with `rx="7"` (≈22% corner radius), filled with Dark Navy. This radius aligns with iOS and Android app icon conventions.

**S-curve path:**
```svg
<path d="M20 9 C20 9 22 9 22 11.5
         C22 14 18 14.5 16 14.5
         C14 14.5 10 15 10 17.5
         C10 20 12 21 14 21"
      fill="none" stroke="#4ECDC4" stroke-width="3"
      stroke-linecap="round" opacity="0.9"/>
```
Four chained cubic Bézier curves forming a continuous S-shape. The path starts at the upper-right (20, 9), curves right to form the top bowl of the S, crosses the centre leftward, and curves left to form the bottom bowl, ending at (14, 21). `stroke-linecap="round"` gives soft endpoints. The path has no fill — it's purely a stroked outline, keeping it light and open.

**Slash line:**
```svg
<line x1="20" y1="5" x2="12" y2="27"
      stroke="#E94560" stroke-width="2.5" stroke-linecap="round"/>
```
A straight line from (20, 5) to (12, 27), running from upper-right to lower-left. This gives an angle of approximately 75° from horizontal, matching the brand specification. The stroke is slightly thinner than the S-curve (2.5 vs 3) but the coral colour gives it more visual weight. It extends beyond the S-curve's bounds (y: 5–27 vs 9–21), asserting dominance in the composition.

**Encryption dots:**
```svg
<circle cx="7"  cy="7"  r="1" fill="#8892A0" opacity="0.3"/>
<circle cx="7"  cy="11" r="1" fill="#8892A0" opacity="0.2"/>
<circle cx="25" cy="21" r="1" fill="#8892A0" opacity="0.3"/>
<circle cx="25" cy="25" r="1" fill="#8892A0" opacity="0.2"/>
```
Four 1px-radius circles in Slate grey at low opacity, placed in the top-left and bottom-right corners. They form two vertical pairs, mirrored diagonally. The alternating opacities (0.3 and 0.2) create a subtle pulse effect. At small sizes (16px) they disappear gracefully, which is intentional — they're a detail that rewards closer inspection.

## 7. Scalability

The icon was designed with progressive simplification in mind:

| Size | What's Visible | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| **512px** | All elements: S-curve, slash, dots, rounded corners | Full detail, suitable for app stores and marketing |
| **64px** | S-curve, slash, corners; dots barely visible | Standard app icon size |
| **32px** | S-curve and slash clearly readable; dots as texture | Standard favicon |
| **16px** | Slash dominates; S-curve provides shape context; dots gone | Minimum viable size — the slash alone carries recognition |

## 8. Relationship to Brand Guidelines

This logo implementation aligns with the SG/Send Design Guidelines (v0.3.5) as follows:

| Guideline Requirement | Implementation |
|-----------------------|----------------|
| The slash is the brand mark | Slash is the most prominent element in both icon and wordmark |
| Slash at 75° from horizontal | Icon slash: ~75° (from coordinate geometry); wordmark slash: exactly 75° (computed) |
| Slash weight 1.2× letterforms | Wordmark: stroke calculated at 1.2× DM Sans Bold's estimated stroke width |
| Teal is the hero colour | Used for S-curve (icon), slash (wordmark), and as the single accent |
| Coral for "what we never see" | Used for the slash in the icon — the encryption boundary |
| DM Sans as brand typeface | Wordmark uses DM Sans Bold 700 and Medium 500 |
| Logo is purely typographic | The wordmark follows this; the icon adds a symbolic layer for small-format contexts |
| Never use teal and coral adjacent | In the icon they intersect (slash crosses S-curve) but don't sit side-by-side — the navy background separates them |
