# Brief: Write Outreach Messages for SG/Send Challenge Campaign

**Context for the LLM:** You are helping me write personal outreach messages to friends and contacts, asking them to try my product SG/Send. I need multiple variants for different channels and languages. Please read the full context below before writing anything.

---

## About Me

I'm the founder/builder of SG/Send. I'm reaching out to personal contacts — friends, ex-colleagues, LinkedIn connections, people I know. This is NOT cold outreach to strangers. The tone should be personal, honest, and low-pressure. I hate the "hey friend, buy my product" approach. I want something that feels genuine.

## About SG/Send

SG/Send is encrypted file transfer. You upload a file in your browser, it gets encrypted with AES-256-GCM before it leaves your device, and you get a link to share. The recipient clicks the link and decrypts in their browser. The server only ever stores encrypted data — we can't read the files even if we wanted to. That's what "zero-knowledge encryption" means.

- **Website:** https://sgraph.ai (live, multilingual — 12 languages, 17 country variants)
- **Product:** https://send.sgraph.ai
- **Buy page:** https://buy.stripe.com/fZu8wHdv23nN1Z85jn5os02
- **Price:** £5 for 500 encrypted file transfers (£0.01 per file)
- **It's open source**
- **Legal entity:** The Cyber Boardroom Ltd (UK)
- **Tagline:** "Your files, your keys, your privacy"

## The Campaign: "The SG/Send Challenge"

Instead of asking people to buy my product, I'm framing it as a challenge with a reward:

**The challenge:**
1. Go to sgraph.ai
2. Find the pricing/buy page
3. Buy £5 of credits via Stripe
4. Use your credits to send an encrypted file
5. Get someone to open/download it
6. Send me a message THROUGH SG/Send confirming you did it

**The reward:** I send them a £5 Amazon voucher back (via SG/Send), so they're net-zero financially. I'm compensating them for their time, not asking them to spend money.

**Why this framing works:**
- It's a challenge/game, not a sales pitch
- It tests the full user journey (discovery → payment → usage → sharing)
- Step 6 forces a second use of the product
- The voucher arrives via SG/Send, demonstrating value delivery
- I learn where people get stuck (which IS the point)

## Tone Guidelines

**DO:**
- Sound like me talking to a friend
- Be honest ("I built this thing and I need people to test the full flow")
- Be specific about what I'm asking (not vague)
- Mention the Amazon voucher naturally (it's a thank-you, not a bribe)
- Keep it light

**DON'T:**
- Sound like a startup pitch deck
- Use words like "revolutionary", "game-changing", "disrupting"
- Be apologetic ("sorry to bother you...")
- Over-explain the encryption (they'll see it on the site)
- Write walls of text
- Use emojis excessively (one or two is fine)
