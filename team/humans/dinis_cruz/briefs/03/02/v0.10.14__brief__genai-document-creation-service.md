## The Opportunity

As of March 2026, the ability to go from voice memos, transcripts, rough documents, and half-baked ideas to polished slide decks, infographics, notebooks, and reports has reached a tipping point. The tools exist. The models are good enough. But most people don't know about them, don't know how to use them, don't know how to prompt effectively, and don't have the time or taste to do it well.

This is the photo printing analogy: in the early days of digital photography, everyone had photos on their computer, but not everyone had the printer, the paper, the software, or the inclination to turn them into printed cards. Businesses started in bedrooms — take an order on a website, print it on a normal printer, ship it by post. The technology was commodity. The value was in the service: turning what the customer had into what the customer wanted.

The same opportunity exists now for GenAI content creation.

---

## What the Service Does

**Input:** Whatever the customer has — voice memos, interview transcripts, rough documents, half-written decks, scattered notes, ideas in their head.

**Output:** Polished, professional materials:
- Documents and reports
- Slide decks and presentations
- Infographics
- NotebookLM-style audio briefings
- Newsletters and summaries
- Multilingual variants of any of the above

**The process:** A combination of human expertise (framing, questioning, taste, iteration) and GenAI tools (Claude, ChatGPT, NotebookLM, Llama, image generation, etc.) — whichever model best serves the task.

---

## Why Now, Not Six Months Ago

Six months ago, doing this required significant manual effort and the output quality was inconsistent. Now:

- Models produce significantly better first drafts
- NotebookLM and similar tools create compelling audio and visual outputs
- Slide deck generation is approaching "good enough for business use"
- Infographic generation is viable
- The workflow from voice → transcript → structured document → polished output is proven (this is literally what the human does every day with these memos)

**The proof:** This briefing process — voice memo → Otter.ai transcript → Claude analysis → structured brief — is the service in miniature. The human is already doing this daily. The question is: can other people pay for it?

---

## Where the Human Adds Value

The service is NOT "we run your text through an LLM." Anyone can do that. The value is in what humans bring:

**The art of questioning.** The right questions extract the right content. A skilled interviewer gets better material from 30 minutes than an unskilled one gets from three hours. This is not a formula — it's micro-decisions about what to probe, what to skip, what to reframe.

**Framing and taste.** Knowing what makes a good slide deck vs. a mediocre one. Knowing when the infographic needs to be simpler. Knowing when the document needs more depth vs. more clarity. This is editorial judgment that LLMs don't have.

**Brand and trust.** People are buying the person, the insights, the perspective — not just the output. A document produced by a known expert carries more weight than the same document produced anonymously by an LLM.

**Iteration and discovery.** The most valuable phase is often not the final output — it's the process of getting there. The conversation that clarifies thinking. The draft that reveals what's missing. The feedback loop that sharpens the message. The service enables discovery, not just production.

## The Multilingual Angle

This is a massive market differentiator. If people in English-speaking markets are underserved by these capabilities, non-English markets are dramatically more underserved:

- Many GenAI tools work well in multiple languages but the awareness is lower outside English-speaking communities
- A NotebookLM-style audio briefing in Portuguese or German or Japanese is striking for someone who hasn't seen it
- Multilingual output from a single input (brief me in English, deliver in five languages) is genuinely powerful
- The SG/Send multilingual infrastructure (17 variants already live) is a foundation for this

**The pitch to non-English markets:** "Give me 30 minutes of your time in your language. I'll give you back a polished deck, a report, and an audio briefing — in your language, at a quality level you haven't seen before."

## The Phased Approach (Wardley Map Thinking)

The key insight: **build this on top of usage, not ahead of it.** Solve the right problem at the right time. Don't build the platform before validating the market.

### Phase 1: Manual Service (Now)

Everything done with commodity tools that exist today:

| Step | Tool | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Customer interview | Zoom / phone / WhatsApp voice note | Available |
| Transcription | Otter.ai / Whisper | Available |
| Document creation | Claude / ChatGPT / any LLM | Available |
| Slide deck | Claude + PPTX generation, or manual | Available |
| Infographic | Claude + design tools | Available |
| Audio briefing | NotebookLM | Available |
| Delivery | Email / SG/Send | Available |
| Billing | Stripe | Available |

**The model:** Charge an hourly consulting rate or per-deliverable rate. The customer gets the output. The human does the work using off-the-shelf tools.

**The key question Phase 1 answers:** Will customers pay for this? At what price point? What do they actually want?

**Price positioning:** Don't overpromise. At £50-500 per deliverable, the quality expectation is manageable. At £20,000, it's not. Align with current LLM output quality. The models will get better — your margins improve over time without raising prices.

### Phase 2: Add Technology (Storage, Website, Workflow)

Introduce SG/Send infrastructure:

- **Upload portal:** Customer uploads documents, voice memos, transcripts to a simple website
- **Secure storage:** Files stored in SG/Send vault (encrypted, auditable)
- **Multilingual:** Offer output in multiple languages from day one
- **Billing:** Consumption-based via Stripe (already wired up)

This is where SG/Send adds value beyond "person using LLMs":
- Customer data never leaves an encrypted environment
- Every edit, every version, every decision has provenance
- The vault becomes the project workspace

### Phase 3: Automation

Start automating the repeatable parts:

- Auto-transcription on upload
- Auto-first-draft generation (human reviews and refines)
- Template-based slide deck generation
- Automated multilingual variants
- Customer-facing UI for reviewing and requesting revisions

The human's role shifts from "doing the work" to "reviewing and refining the work" — dramatically increasing throughput.

### Phase 4: Full Platform

- Self-service for simple jobs (upload transcript → get deck)
- Human-assisted for complex jobs (strategy sessions, interview-based content)
- Custom workflows per customer
- Private vaults for corporate customers (data never leaves their environment)
- Proof of ownership, editing history, signatures at every step

## The Self-Reinforcing Market

Anyone who uses these techniques becomes a future customer, because they raise the bar:

1. Executive sees a polished AI-generated deck for the first time
2. "All our presentations should look like this"
3. The team now needs to produce at this quality level regularly
4. They either learn the tools themselves (hard, time-consuming) or use the service (easy, immediate)
5. As they scale, they need their own environment → SG/Send enterprise deployment
