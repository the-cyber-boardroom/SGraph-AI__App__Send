## 1. Executive Summary

This document establishes **SGraph-AI** as the client-facing SaaS product brand, distinct from **MGraph-AI** (backend infrastructure services). The first product, previously codenamed "Secure Send", will launch as **SGraph Send** at `send.sgraph.ai`.

The core argument: "Secure Send" was always a working title describing a feature. **SGraph Send** is a brand â€” one that belongs to a coherent product family, owns its namespace, avoids a crowded competitive landscape, and tells a story about what connects these products together.

## 2. Why Not "Secure Send"

### 2.1 The Name Is Saturated

Research across Google, app stores, and trademark databases reveals the "send + secure/safe" namespace is one of the most crowded in the file sharing space:

| Existing Product | Domain | Description |
|-----------------|--------|-------------|
| **XM SendSecure** | xmedius.com | Enterprise secure file exchange by OpenText. SDKs in 6 languages. Google Play app. Established enterprise customer base. |
| **SendSecure.io** | sendsecure.io | P2P secure file transfer service. Listed on Capterra. |
| **sendsecure.ly** | sendsecure.ly | Single-use secret sharing links. Essentially our one-time secrets feature. |
| **SendSafely** | sendsafely.com | End-to-end encryption platform. HIPAA, GDPR, CCPA compliant. Integrations with Salesforce, Zendesk, Intercom. Significant LinkedIn presence. |
| **SendSafe** | sendsafe.app | "Send secrets to others, safely." Encrypted, deleted upon retrieval. Developer-focused. |
| **SendSafe** (MailHippo) | mailhippo.com | Registered trademark. HIPAA-compliant encrypted email. `sendsafe.to` email addresses. |
| **sendsafe.net** | sendsafe.net | "Share data up to 5GB." Active service. |
| **SendSafe** (app) | App Store | Shopping/delivery app with escrow payments. |

Every combination of "send" + "secure/safe/safely/securely" is taken across `.com`, `.io`, `.ly`, `.app`, and `.net`. Even if `sendsecure.ai` or `sendsafe.ai` domains are available, the brand would be perpetually confused with 5+ existing products.

### 2.2 Descriptive Names Are Weak Brands

"Secure Send" describes a feature â€” it tells you what the product does, not what it is. The strongest product names in tech are not descriptive:

| Product | Describes What It Does? | Brand Strength |
|---------|------------------------|----------------|
| Dropbox | No ("box" for "drops"?) | Iconic â€” the word now means cloud storage |
| Stripe | No (a stripe of what?) | Iconic â€” the word now means online payments |
| Slack | No (the opposite of productive) | Iconic â€” the word now means team chat |
| WeTransfer | Partially | Moderate â€” recognisable but generic root |
| Zoom | No (speed metaphor) | Iconic â€” the word now means video calls |
| Signal | Partially | Strong â€” implies communication + clarity |

Descriptive names like "CloudFileStore" or "SecureSend" are immediately understandable but immediately forgettable. They describe the feature, not the identity. They also can't grow â€” if "Secure Send" later adds LLM features, semantic graphs, or image tools, the name actively misleads.

### 2.3 SEO Is a Losing Battle

Searching "secure send" returns a wall of competing products, generic security articles, and email encryption guides. Achieving first-page ranking for "secure send" would require competing against:

- OpenText's enterprise SEO budget (XM SendSecure)
- SendSafely's established domain authority
- Hundreds of "how to send files securely" content articles
- Generic email security product pages

By contrast, "sgraph" returns mostly graph library references and academic papers. The search landscape is wide open. From day one, `sgraph.ai` can own its name on Google.

### 2.4 Trademark Risk

"SendSecure" and "SendSafe" are both actively used product names by commercial entities (OpenText and MailHippo respectively). While "Secure Send" (reversed word order) may not infringe, operating in the exact same product category with a near-identical name invites unnecessary legal friction and customer confusion. SGraph carries zero trademark risk in the file sharing space.

## 3. Why "SGraph Send" at `send.sgraph.ai`

### 3.1 The "S" Is Multi-Valent

The genius of "SGraph" is that the "S" doesn't mean just one thing. It means all of these simultaneously, and different products in the portfolio can lean into whichever meaning fits:

| S-meaning | Relevance | Which products lean into it |
|-----------|-----------|----------------------------|
| **Secure** | Zero-knowledge encryption, privacy-first | Send, all products |
| **Semantic** | Knowledge graphs, LLM-driven analysis, meaning extraction | Semantic Text, Send (LLM features) |
| **Send** | File transfer, sharing, delivery | Send |
| **Share** | Collaboration, distribution | Send, Image |
| **Simple** | Minimal, focused, no bloat | All products (core design principle) |
| **Service** | SaaS delivery model | All products |

This ambiguity is a strength, not a weakness. It gives the brand room to grow. "Secure Send" can only ever be about secure sending. "SGraph" can be about anything that's secure, semantic, shared, or simple.

### 3.2 It Owns Its Namespace

| Search Term | Results Today | Opportunity |
|------------|--------------|-------------|
| "sgraph" | Graph libraries, academic papers | Wide open â€” no competing SaaS products |
| "sgraph.ai" | Nothing | Completely uncontested |
| "sgraph send" | Nothing | Completely uncontested |
| "send.sgraph.ai" | Nothing | Completely uncontested |

From day one, every search for "sgraph" points to us. There's no SEO battle. No brand confusion. No trademark overlap. The name is clean.

### 3.3 It Creates a Product Family

A single product is a tool. A product family is a platform. SGraph-AI immediately communicates that there's a coherent ecosystem:


A name like "Secure Send" has no family. It's an orphan product. Adding "Secure Image" or "Secure Text" sounds like a security company, not a SaaS portfolio.

### 3.4 It Connects to the Graph Ecosystem

The "Graph" in SGraph ties directly to the underlying technology:

| Layer | Brand | What It Does |

The naming tells a truthful story about the architecture: SGraph products are built on MGraph services. The "Graph" suffix is the family name. The prefix letter differentiates the purpose: M for meta/infrastructure, S for secure/semantic/SaaS, F for financial.

### 3.5 `send.sgraph.ai` Reads Naturally

The full URL reads as a sentence: **"send (via) sgraph.ai"** â€” or expanded: **"Send Secure Graph AI."**

In conversation:

It works verbally. It works in chat. It works in documentation. Compare:

> "Check send.sgraph for the link" â† clean  

### 3.6 The Domain Is Already Owned

No acquisition cost. No negotiation. No risk of losing the name to a squatter. The domain `sgraph.ai` is already under control and ready to deploy. This alone removes weeks of naming research, domain negotiation, and trademark clearance from the critical path.

## 9. The SGraph Portfolio â€” Product Positioning

### 9.1 SGraph Send (`send.sgraph.ai`)

**One-liner:** Encrypted file sharing. Zero knowledge. Zero fuss.

**What it is:** A privacy-first file sharing service where files are encrypted in the browser before upload. The server never sees your content. Share files, text, and secrets with anyone â€” they auto-delete when done.

**Key differentiators:** Zero-knowledge encryption, transparency panel (shows users exactly what we capture), ephemeral by default, deploy-anywhere, plugin architecture, LLM integration, credit-based billing, open source.

**Target users:** Privacy-conscious individuals, developers (CLI/SDK), small teams, enterprises (data rooms, branded deployments).

### 9.2 SGraph Image (`image.sgraph.ai`)

**One-liner:** Image manipulation. Private. Programmable.

**What it is:** An image processing service (resize, crop, convert, watermark, optimise, AI-enhance) that processes images client-side or via encrypted server-side pipelines. The same zero-knowledge principles: your images are your images.

**Key differentiators:** Privacy-first image processing, API-driven, graph-based transformation pipelines (built on MGraph-AI__Service__Html__Graph), batch processing, CLI/SDK.

**Target users:** Developers, content teams, anyone who needs image manipulation without uploading to a third-party service that harvests their content.

### 9.3 SGraph Text (`text.sgraph.ai`)

**One-liner:** Semantic text generation. Structured. Private.

**What it is:** A text generation and analysis service powered by LLMs with a graph-based semantic engine underneath. Generate summaries, extract entities, build knowledge graphs, produce structured content â€” all with the same zero-knowledge model.

**Key differentiators:** Semantic graph output (not just flat text), privacy-first (BYOK LLM keys or in-browser processing), structured data extraction, built on MGraph-AI__Service__Semantic_Text.

**Target users:** Researchers, analysts, content teams, developers building NLP pipelines.

### 9.4 SGraph Billing (`billing.sgraph.ai`)

**One-liner:** Usage-based billing. Simple. Transparent.

**What it is:** A billing and credit management service that powers the economics of all SGraph products. Buy credits, track usage, manage subscriptions. Also available as an embeddable billing engine for other SaaS products.

**Key differentiators:** Credit-based consumption model, per-action cost tracking, transparent pricing, Stripe integration, embeddable.

**Target users:** SGraph product users (internal), other SaaS builders who want a drop-in billing service (external).
