## The Idea

Create a musician-specific UI and workflow experience on the SG/Send website. This is the first example of a segment-specific customization: not just multi-language (Portuguese, Spanish, etc.) but multi-audience. The same underlying technology, presented through a lens that speaks to how musicians actually work.

Musicians are an ideal audience because:

- They collaborate constantly (multiple musicians, producers, mix engineers)
- They produce versioned content (drafts, mixes, remixes, masters)
- They have never experienced proper version control (their versioning is `Song_X_final_v3_ACTUALLY_FINAL_mix2b.mp3`)
- They share sensitive pre-release material that needs protection
- They work across devices (studio desktop, laptop on the road, phone for quick review)

## Wardley Maps of a Song

The production of a song maps naturally to the Explorer/Villager/Town Planner evolution:

### Genesis / Explorer Phase

The idea. Rough notes, voice memos, draft lyrics, initial chord progressions, demo recordings. One or two people. Messy, creative, fast-moving.

**SG/Send primitive:** A simple folder (or vault) with a loose structure. Maybe just a flat collection of files. Auto-commit captures every iteration. The songwriter never loses a version, even if they do not think about saving.

**What the UI shows:** A simple workspace. Drop files in. Play audio files inline. Add notes. Everything is captured.

### Productisation / Villager Phase

Multiple people involved. The producer, session musicians, the mix engineer. Multiple tracks, multiple takes, multiple versions of the mix. This is where collaboration gets complex and where the `_final_v3_ACTUALLY_FINAL` filename problem appears.

**SG/Send primitive:** A vault with branches. Each musician or producer works on their own branch. The lead producer's branch is "main." Musicians commit their takes to their branches. The producer merges what they want into main. Outtakes are preserved on the musician's branch (there is gold in outtakes).

**What the UI shows:** Branch-per-musician. The producer sees all branches. They can listen to any musician's latest work, compare takes side by side (play two audio files in parallel), and merge the ones they want. The merge UI does not say "merge." It says "use this take" or "combine these."

Auto-commit means every recording session is captured. The desktop client syncs the studio folder. Every time Logic or Pro Tools saves, the vault commits. No manual save-to-vault step.

### Commodity / Town Planner Phase

The final master. Artwork. Liner notes. Distribution metadata. Remix stems. The materials that go to the label, the distributor, the streaming platforms. Everything is locked down. Final versions only.

**SG/Send primitive:** A vault (or a separate vault) with the final, signed materials. The master audio is signed by the producer. The artwork is signed by the designer. The metadata is signed by whoever manages distribution. Everything is versioned and nothing is overwritten.

**What the UI shows:** A polished package view. Album artwork, track listing, play buttons. This is what gets shared with the label or the distributor. A vault that looks like an album, not a file system.

## Musician-Specific Viewers

Custom Web Components for music workflows:

| Component | Function |
|---|---|
| `<sg-audio-player>` | Play audio files inline. Waveform visualisation. Seek, loop, A/B compare. |
| `<sg-multi-track>` | Play multiple audio files in parallel. Sync playback. Solo/mute individual tracks. |
| `<sg-lyrics-viewer>` | Display lyrics alongside audio playback. Timestamped if possible. |
| `<sg-take-compare>` | Side-by-side comparison of two takes of the same part. "Pick this one" button. |
| `<sg-album-view>` | Final package view: artwork, track list, play buttons, metadata. |

These are JavaScript-driven UIs that are straightforward to build. Audio playback is native in browsers. Waveform rendering is well-supported by libraries. The complexity is in the workflow design, not the technology.

## The Three Primitives for Musicians

| Primitive | Music Use Case |
|---|---|
| Send a file | Share a rough demo. Send a mix for review. Share a single take. Can be set to play-once-and-delete for pre-release protection. |
| Send a folder/zip | Share all materials for a session. A zip of stems, a folder of takes, a collection of reference tracks. The folder view lets the recipient browse and play without downloading everything. |
| Vault | The ongoing collaboration workspace. Branches per musician. Auto-commit from the studio desktop. Merge when the producer is happy. Full history of every version. |

## Pre-Packaged Workflows

When a musician visits the site ("Are you a musician? Here are your workflows"), they get pre-packaged vault templates:

- **Solo songwriter:** Simple vault, one branch, auto-commit, notes + audio + lyrics
- **Band collaboration:** Vault with branch-per-musician, shared main branch, producer as merge authority
- **Album production:** Master vault with inner vaults per song, final package view, distribution-ready structure
- **Remix project:** Fork a vault, work on your branch, submit back to the original producer

Each template comes with a folder structure, suggested workflow, and the musician-specific viewers loaded by default.

## Multi-Cultural Angle

This extends the existing i18n work. The musician UI is not just in English. It is in Portuguese (Brazil has a massive music market), Spanish (Latin America), and eventually other languages. The UI copy, the workflow descriptions, and the viewer labels are all translatable. The music is universal. The interface speaks their language.
