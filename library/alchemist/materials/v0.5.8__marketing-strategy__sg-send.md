## Executive Summary

SG/Send's marketing strategy is built on three pillars: **open-source credibility** (the security community validates us), **product-led growth** (every recipient is a potential sender), and **content authority** (we teach, not sell). The budget is 10% of the raise (£30K), and every pound must produce measurable results.

**Early traction validates demand:** 30 paying customers in 30 days from first commit (8 Feb → 10 Mar 2026), zero churn. The product-led growth loop is working before marketing spend. Built on 2 years of open source infrastructure (osbot-utils, osbot-aws, osbot-fast-api).

This is not a brand marketing strategy. It is a customer acquisition strategy designed to scale from 30 to 300+ customers:
1. ~~Pilot customers (5-10 in 3 months)~~ **ACHIEVED: 30 in 30 days**
2. Developer adoption (CLI/SDK usage)
3. Conference visibility (security and legal tech communities)
4. Organic traffic (content that ranks)

## 1. Positioning

### The Core Position

**For** professionals and enterprises who share confidential files,
**SG/Send is** a zero-knowledge encrypted transfer service
**that** encrypts in the browser, never sees the plaintext, and requires no app or account for the recipient.
**Unlike** email (no encryption), WeTransfer (server reads files), and Intralinks (provider holds keys),
**SG/Send** is end-to-end encrypted, recipient-frictionless, open source, self-hostable, and PKI-enabled.

### The Taglines (Audience-Specific)

| Audience | Tagline |
|---|---|
| **Everyone** | "The server never sees your files." |
| **Security professionals** | "Signal for the web." |
| **Developers** | "Encrypted file transfer in 5 lines of code." |
| **Enterprise / legal** | "Zero-knowledge. Self-hostable. Verifiable." |
| **Investors** | "Profitable by design. No kill date." |

### What We Are Not

- We are **not** a cloud storage product (Dropbox, Google Drive)
- We are **not** a messaging app (Signal, WhatsApp)
- We are **not** a full data room (Intralinks) — yet
- We are **not** a password manager (Bitwarden, 1Password)

We are a **transfer tool.** Send, receive, done. The simplicity is the feature.

## 2. The Three Marketing Pillars

### Pillar 1: Open-Source Credibility

**Principle:** In zero-knowledge encryption, trust is the product. Open source is the proof.

**Actions:**

| Action | Cost | Timeline | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| GitHub repository maintenance | £0 (founder time) | Ongoing | Stars, forks, contributor PRs |
| Apache 2.0 licence prominently displayed | £0 | Done | — |
| Security architecture documentation public | £0 | Month 1 | Cited in security discussions |
| Hacker News launch post | £0 | Month 1-2 | Front page, comment quality |
| Security researcher outreach | £500 (bug bounty seed) | Month 2 | Responsible disclosures received |
| OWASP community engagement | £500 (sponsorship) | Month 3 | Talk accepted or referenced |

**Why this works:** The security community is the most valuable marketing channel for an encryption product. A security researcher saying "I reviewed the code and the crypto is correct" is worth more than any advertising campaign.

### Pillar 2: Product-Led Growth

**Principle:** Every recipient of an encrypted file discovers SG/Send. The product markets itself.

**The viral loop:**

```
Sender encrypts a file
  → Recipient receives a link
    → Recipient decrypts in browser (first experience of SG/Send)
      → Recipient becomes a sender (5-10% conversion hypothesis)
        → Their recipients discover SG/Send
          → Loop continues
```

**Actions:**

| Action | Cost | Timeline | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|
| "Powered by SG/Send" branding on free tier | £0 | Launch | Brand impressions per transfer |
| One-time secrets feature (viral acquisition) | £5,000 (development) | Month 2 | Secrets created/day |
| Referral programme (credits for referrals) | £2,000 (credits budget) | Month 3 | Referral conversion rate |
| Public transparency panel | £0 | Done | Trust signal for new users |
| Sender-to-recipient conversion tracking | £1,000 (analytics) | Month 2 | Conversion rate by channel |

**Why this works:** SG/Send's "no app, no account" design means every transfer is a product demo for the recipient. The friction of signup is zero — you just use a browser. This is the same dynamic that made Dropbox and Calendly viral.

## 7. The Marketing Principles

1. **Teach, don't sell.** Content that educates the market creates demand for the product. We earn trust by explaining the problem; we earn revenue by solving it.

2. **The product is the marketing.** Every encrypted transfer is a product demo for the recipient. The viral loop is the primary acquisition channel.

3. **Open source is the trust signal.** In zero-knowledge encryption, "anyone can verify" is the strongest marketing claim possible. Marketing amplifies this; it does not replace it.

4. **Security community first.** A CISO's endorsement is worth more than a million impressions. We earn credibility by being correct, not by being loud.

5. **Hypothesis-driven spending.** Every marketing pound has a testable hypothesis and a measurable outcome. We discover what works; we don't guess.

6. **No hype.** "The gold is real." We present what we've built, not what we plan to build. Credibility is the brand.

3. **Open source enables community marketing.** Contributors become advocates. Bug reporters become evangelists.
4. **"Signal for the web" is sticky.** One phrase communicates the entire value proposition.
