## Summary

Dinis Cruz is a security industry veteran with 20+ years of experience in application security, open-source leadership, and enterprise security architecture. He is the founder and sole human operator of SGraph Send (SG/Send), a zero-knowledge encrypted file transfer service that he builds and operates using 19 specialised AI agent roles.

His career has been defined by a single conviction: **security should be built in, not bolted on.** SG/Send is the product expression of that conviction — encryption that is architectural, not optional; zero-knowledge that is mathematical, not contractual; and transparency that is structural, not performative.

**The compound launch:** Dinis spent 2 years building the open source infrastructure (osbot-utils, osbot-aws, osbot-fast-api) that powers SG/Send, developed across prior startups. First commit to SG/Send: 8 February 2026. 30 paying customers: 10 March 2026. 30 days from zero to revenue — not a cold start, but the culmination of years of technical groundwork.

## Why This Founder for This Product

### 1. Domain Expertise Is the Moat

SG/Send is not a generic file sharing tool. It is a cryptographic architecture. The founder's 20+ years in application security means:

- **Threat models are instinctive.** The architecture was designed to eliminate classes of attack, not to patch individual vulnerabilities.
- **Security review is native.** The 25 security findings catalogued in 3 days reflect a security-first culture that comes from the founder, not from a compliance checklist.
- **The "correct implementation" quote matters.** When a 25-year security veteran says the cryptography is correct, it's because the founder knows what correct looks like.

### 2. The Agentic Team Is a Founder Capability

The 19 AI agent roles are not a generic AI experiment. They are a structured engineering team with:

- Formal role definitions (10-30KB each)
- Defined responsibilities, collaboration patterns, and quality gates
- Three teams (Explorer, Villager, Town Planner) operating at different paces
- 200+ versioned documents produced in 9 days

This works because the founder has the domain expertise to direct, review, and quality-gate every output. The AI amplifies; the human ensures correctness. Without the founder's security expertise, the agentic team would produce volume without validity.

### 3. Speed Is a Founder Trait

## The Operating Model

### How One Person Runs a 19-Role Engineering Team

The founder operates as the human decision-maker, quality gate, and external interface for a structured AI team:

| Responsibility | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Decision-making** | All architectural, product, and business decisions are made by the founder |
| **Quality review** | Every document, code commit, and deliverable is reviewed before entering the system |
| **External relations** | All investor, customer, and partner interactions are human-to-human |
| **Direction setting** | Daily briefs provide priorities and context to the agentic team |
| **Role management** | Each of 19 roles has a comprehensive definition the founder maintains |

### What This Means for Investors

- **Single founder risk is mitigated by institutional memory.** 200+ documents in git mean the project's knowledge survives any session. A co-founder or hire can onboard by reading the repository.
- **Capital efficiency is structural.** The operating burn rate is a fraction of a comparable human team. This is not lean — it is a new operating model.
- **Quality is not sacrificed for speed.** The 25 security findings, architecture reviews, and incident response demonstrate enterprise-grade engineering discipline.

## What the Founder Needs

### A Co-Founder

The operating model is proven for building. It needs a second human for:

- **Business development** — identifying and closing enterprise customers
- **Sales** — converting pilot customers to paying contracts
- **Product management** — translating customer needs into product requirements
- **Fundraising** — managing investor relationships at scale

The ideal co-founder has experience in enterprise SaaS sales, understands regulated industries (legal, financial services, healthcare), and is comfortable with the agentic team model.

### Why Not Hire Instead?

The agentic team model means SG/Send does not need a traditional engineering team. What it needs is a second human with complementary skills — someone who sells while the founder builds. A co-founder, not an employee.
