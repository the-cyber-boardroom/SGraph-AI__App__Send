## Executive Summary

SG/Send's seed round (£300K for 20%) is designed for a specific type of investor: individuals or small groups who understand security, value capital efficiency, and want to back a provably real technology at the lowest possible entry point.

This document defines five investor categories, explains what each brings beyond capital, and provides a framework for prioritising conversations.

## 2. The Five Investor Categories

### Category A: Security Industry Angels

**Who they are:** CISOs, security consultants, penetration testers, security tool founders — people who understand zero-knowledge encryption, PKI, and threat models.

**Why they invest:**
- They understand the technical moat intuitively. "Browser-based PKI with non-extractable keys" is not a buzzword — it's a specific architectural achievement they can evaluate.
- They have lived the problem. They've sent confidential documents via email knowing it was wrong.
- They can validate the cryptographic claims — and they know that open source is the only way to make those claims credible.

**What they bring beyond capital:**
- Technical validation (their endorsement is a trust signal for other investors)
- Customer introductions (their clients are SG/Send's target market)
- Security advisory input (practical threat modelling, not theoretical)
- Conference and community access (BSides, OWASP, Black Hat network)

**Ideal check size:** £20K-50K
**Priority:** **Highest.** These investors become evangelists.

**Example profile:** A CISO at a mid-market firm who has budget authority and sends term sheets, incident reports, and board materials weekly. They invest because they would use the product — and they'd deploy it for their team.

---

### Category B: Fintech / Legal Tech Angels

**Who they are:** Founders and operators in financial services technology, legal technology, or regulatory technology. They understand regulated industries, enterprise sales cycles, and compliance requirements.

**Why they invest:**
- They know the customer. Investment firms, law firms, and compliance teams are their daily counterparties.
- They understand data room economics. They know Intralinks charges £3,000-10,000/month and that the encryption is provider-controlled.
- They see the enterprise upsell path: encrypted transfer → managed instance → data room → enterprise SLA.

**What they bring beyond capital:**
- Direct customer introductions (investment firms, law firms)
- Enterprise sales guidance (pricing, procurement, compliance requirements)
- Industry-specific product feedback (what features close deals vs. what's nice-to-have)
- Regulatory navigation (GDPR, FCA, SOX compliance knowledge)

**Ideal check size:** £30K-50K
**Priority:** **High.** These investors accelerate customer acquisition.

**Example profile:** A former VP at a legal tech company who has a network of 50+ law firms. They invest because they've seen firms pay £10K/month for data rooms with weaker encryption.

---

### Category C: Open Source / Developer Platform Angels

**Who they are:** Founders or early employees of open-source companies (Redis, Elastic, GitLab, Hasura, PostHog). They understand the open-source business model: community adoption drives enterprise demand.

**Why they invest:**
- They recognise the open-source → enterprise playbook. SG/Send's Apache 2.0 licence, CLI/SDK roadmap, and developer platform strategy mirror proven patterns.
- They understand that "anyone can verify" is a competitive moat, not a vulnerability.
- They see the network effect: CLI tool → developer adoption → enterprise procurement → managed instances.

**What they bring beyond capital:**
- Open-source community strategy (how to build adoption, when to introduce commercial features)
- Developer marketing expertise (conference talks, documentation, community engagement)
- Enterprise conversion playbook (how to turn free users into enterprise contracts)
- Hiring/co-founder network (developer-focused talent)

**Ideal check size:** £20K-40K
**Priority:** **High.** These investors guide the open-source strategy.

**Example profile:** A former Elastic or PostHog early employee who saw the open-source → enterprise journey. They invest because SG/Send has the same structural advantages: real technology, open source, clear enterprise upsell.

---

### Category D: AI / Agentic Workflow Angels

**Who they are:** Founders, operators, and investors in the AI/LLM space who understand agentic teams, Claude/GPT integration, and the MCP (Model Context Protocol) ecosystem.

**Why they invest:**
- They recognise the agentic team model as a genuine innovation — not just "using AI" but structuring an entire company around AI agents.
- They see the LLM integration opportunity: "Claude, send this securely" is the first encrypted transfer tool built for the AI-native workflow.
- They understand MCP Server integration as a distribution channel — every AI agent that can send encrypted files increases SG/Send's reach.

**What they bring beyond capital:**
- AI/LLM integration guidance (MCP, tool use, agent frameworks)
- Agentic team model validation (their endorsement of the operating model matters)
- LLM ecosystem connections (Anthropic, OpenAI, developer communities)
- Future of work narrative (SG/Send as the exemplar of the 1-human + AI-agents company)

**Ideal check size:** £20K-50K
**Priority:** **Medium-High.** These investors strengthen the AI narrative.

**Example profile:** An early-stage AI fund partner or a founder who built a successful AI tool. They invest because the agentic team model is a thesis they want to validate — and SG/Send is the strongest evidence they've seen.

---

### Category E: Strategic / Institutional Angels

**Who they are:** Individuals who manage small institutional portfolios, family offices, or angel syndicates. They may not have deep domain expertise but they invest systematically in SEIS/EIS-eligible startups.

**Why they invest:**
- SEIS/EIS tax incentives provide structural downside protection (50% income tax relief, tax-free gains, loss relief)
- Diversified portfolio approach — SG/Send is one of several SEIS/EIS investments
- The capital efficiency story resonates: £300K buys 12+ months of development with no permanent hires

**What they bring beyond capital:**
- Syndicate access (they bring other investors)
- SEIS/EIS process expertise (they know the compliance requirements)
- Portfolio connections (they may have portfolio companies that need encrypted transfer)

**Ideal check size:** £30K-50K
**Priority:** **Medium.** Capital is valuable, but domain expertise matters more at seed stage.

**Example profile:** A SEIS/EIS-focused angel syndicate lead who invests £30K-50K in 5-10 startups per year. They invest because the unit economics, tax incentives, and capital efficiency match their thesis.

## 5. Investor Engagement Approach

### The Principle: "The Gold Is Real"

Every investor conversation leads with evidence, not projections:

| What we show | What we don't show |
|---|---|
| Working product at send.sgraph.ai | Revenue forecasts |
| PKI demo (live encryption/decryption) | Market capture projections |
| Security review validation | Hockey stick growth charts |
| 92 commits in 3 days (velocity proof) | Comparable company extrapolations |
| Independent quotes ("killer feature," "foolproof") | Self-authored testimonials |

### The Three-Meeting Sequence

| Meeting | Duration | Purpose | Materials |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Introduction** | 30 min | Show the product. Demo encryption. Share the PKI story. | Elevator pitch deck, live demo |
| **2. Deep dive** | 60 min | Technical architecture. Business model. Operating model. | Business plan, competitive analysis, investment strategy |
| **3. Terms and decision** | 45 min | Investment terms. SEIS/EIS mechanics. Syndicate structure. | Investment strategy, term sheet (draft) |

### Qualification Criteria

An investor is a strong fit if they can answer "yes" to at least two of:

1. "I would use this product myself or deploy it at my company"
2. "I can introduce SG/Send to 3+ potential customers"
3. "I have domain expertise that helps the product win (security, legal, fintech, open source, AI)"
4. "I understand the SEIS/EIS structure and invest regularly"

## 6. Pipeline Tracking

### Pipeline Stages (B0-B10)

Investor relationships are tracked using a 0-10 scale:

| Stage | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **B0** | Identified — name only | Research |
| **B1** | Contacted — intro sent | Follow up |
| **B2** | Aware — responded, knows about SG/Send | Schedule meeting 1 |
| **B3** | Interested — meeting 1 complete | Schedule deep dive |
| **B4** | Engaged — deep dive complete, asking questions | Provide additional materials |
| **B5** | Evaluating — reviewing terms, doing due diligence | Support DD process |
| **B6** | Verbal commitment — "I'm in" | Send term sheet |
| **B7** | Term sheet signed | SEIS/EIS paperwork |
| **B8** | Funds received | Onboarding, share certificates |
| **B9** | Active investor — providing intros, advice | Maintain relationship |
| **B10** | Champion — actively promoting SG/Send | Leverage for next raise |

### Tracking Location

Investor pipeline is tracked in `library/alchemist/investors/pipeline.issues` using the Issues FS format. Each investor has a folder in `library/alchemist/investors/{investor-name}/` for relationship notes, meeting logs, and materials sent.
