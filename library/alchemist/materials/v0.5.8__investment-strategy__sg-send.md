## Executive Summary

SG/Send is raising **£300,000 for 20% equity at a £1.5M post-money valuation**. The company is UK-based and SEIS/EIS eligible. This is a seed round to sponsor specific growth experiments — not to fund open-ended R&D. Each funded project has clear success metrics and no fixed cost commitment.

The investment structure is designed around the principle: **funding sponsors experiments, not headcount.** The agentic team model means SG/Send does not need a traditional engineering team. It needs capital to test hypotheses, acquire first customers, and achieve compliance certifications that unlock enterprise sales.

## 5. SEIS/EIS Tax Incentives

### For UK Investors

SG/Send is structured to qualify for the UK's Seed Enterprise Investment Scheme (SEIS) and Enterprise Investment Scheme (EIS), which provide significant tax relief to investors:

### SEIS (Seed Enterprise Investment Scheme)

| Benefit | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Income tax relief** | 50% of investment amount (invest £20K, get £10K tax relief) |
| **Capital gains exemption** | Gains on SEIS shares are tax-free if held for 3+ years |
| **Capital gains reinvestment** | 50% exemption on gains reinvested from other assets |
| **Loss relief** | If the company fails, effective loss is ~28p per £1 invested |
| **Maximum per investor** | £200,000 per tax year |
| **Company limit** | £250,000 total SEIS funding |

### EIS (Enterprise Investment Scheme)

| Benefit | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Income tax relief** | 30% of investment amount |
| **Capital gains deferral** | Defer gains from other assets by reinvesting in EIS shares |
| **Capital gains exemption** | Gains on EIS shares are tax-free if held for 3+ years |
| **Loss relief** | If the company fails, effective loss is ~38.5p per £1 invested |
| **Maximum per investor** | £1,000,000 per tax year |
| **Company limit** | £5,000,000 per year |

### Effective Risk Reduction

| Scenario | Without SEIS/EIS | With SEIS (50%) | With EIS (30%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Invest £50,000** | £50,000 at risk | £25,000 at risk | £35,000 at risk |
| **Company fails (total loss)** | Lose £50,000 | Lose ~£14,000 (after tax relief + loss relief) | Lose ~£19,250 |
| **Company exits at 5x** | Gain £200,000 (taxed) | Gain £200,000 (tax-free) | Gain £200,000 (tax-free) |
| **Company exits at 10x** | Gain £450,000 (taxed) | Gain £450,000 (tax-free) | Gain £450,000 (tax-free) |

### Returns at Different Exit Multiples (SEIS Investor, £50K Investment)

| Exit Multiple | Gross Return | Net Return (After SEIS Relief) | Effective Multiple |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0x (total loss) | £0 | £36,000 back (tax + loss relief) | **0.72x** |
| 1x (return capital) | £50,000 | £75,000 | **1.50x** |
| 3x | £150,000 | £175,000 | **3.50x** |
| 5x | £250,000 | £275,000 | **5.50x** |
| 10x | £500,000 | £525,000 | **10.50x** |

**The downside is structurally bounded. The upside is uncapped and tax-free.**

## 6. Risk Analysis

### Investment Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| **Single founder** | High | Co-founder search active. Institutional memory (200+ docs in git) means onboarding is fast. Agentic team provides functional breadth. |
| **No revenue** | High | Serverless = near-zero burn. Professional services available now. Pilot customer acquisition is month 1-3 priority. |
| **Technical risk (crypto)** | Medium | Architecture independently validated. 25 security findings catalogued with mitigation plans. Open source allows community audit. |
| **Market timing** | Low | Post-GDPR, post-Schrems II regulatory environment. Enterprise demand for verifiable encryption is increasing. |
| **Competition** | Medium | No competitor has all five properties (E2E + no app + open source + self-hostable + PKI). Architecture is not retrofittable. |
| **Regulatory** | Low | Open source and self-hostable means no single jurisdiction can block. Apache 2.0 is the most permissive standard licence. |

### Why the Downside Is Bounded

1. **Serverless costs pennies when idle.** There is no infrastructure burn eating capital while we find product-market fit.
2. **No permanent hires.** 100% of investment goes to experiments and compliance, not payroll obligations.
3. **SEIS/EIS tax relief.** Effective downside is ~28-38p per £1 invested.
4. **Open source has terminal value.** Even in a worst case, the technology has community value and can be maintained or acquired.
